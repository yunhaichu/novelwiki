# Consequence Chain Check

Use this during beat generation, beat review, chapter brief creation, chapter brief review, and chapter review.

This check prevents chapters from becoming event lists. A chapter should create consequences that force or strongly motivate what comes next.

## Core Rule

A chapter should not be only:

```text
this happened, then this happened, then this happened
```

It should move by consequence:

```text
because this happened, the protagonist chose this;
that choice created this consequence;
therefore the next action becomes necessary or more costly.
```

## Required Fields

For each chapter or beat, identify:

```text
Opening state:
Protagonist action:
Immediate consequence:
Who reacts:
What becomes harder:
What path closes:
What new path opens:
What must happen next because of this chapter:
If this chapter is removed, what later event collapses:
```

## Review Rules

Mark `REVISE` if:

- deleting the chapter does not affect later events;
- the chapter only adds information but does not change action pressure;
- the ending does not force or strongly motivate a next action;
- events can be freely reordered without changing causality;
- the protagonist acts but no one or nothing responds;
- the chapter reads as `and then` instead of `therefore`, `as a result`, or `instead`.

## Valid Consequences

A consequence can be:

- a new risk;
- a narrowed option;
- a relationship shift;
- an enemy or system response;
- a resource gained or lost;
- a false path eliminated;
- a clue that changes the proof chain;
- a public reaction;
- a deadline becoming tighter;
- a method failing;
- a secret becoming harder to hide;
- a prior detail changing meaning.

## Weak Consequences

These are usually too weak unless paired with another consequence:

```text
protagonist feels confused
reader learns a vague mystery exists
a mood becomes darker
a symbolic line appears
someone says this is important
```

## Output Block For Reviews

When reviewing, include:

```text
Consequence chain:
- Opening state:
- Protagonist action:
- Immediate consequence:
- Who reacts:
- What becomes harder:
- What path closes:
- What new path opens:
- What must happen next:
- Removability risk:
```
