# Anti Record-Driven Plot

Use this rule during chapter design, drafting, review, and final polish.

The purpose is to prevent the story from being driven by systems, reports, logs, databases, notifications, case numbers, screens, or automatic workflow changes.

Modern, sci-fi, office, mystery, crime, school, hospital, insurance, platform, and cyber settings often need records. Records are allowed. But records must not replace people, objects, relationships, and scene action.

## Core Principle

```text
Plot may use records, but must not be driven by records.
Organizations may use process, but must appear through people and scene action.
Evidence may be electronic, but protagonist advantage must become usable in a scene.
```

## Priority Order For Plot Carriers

When designing a chapter, use this priority order:

1. Person-to-person conflict.
2. Physical scene action.
3. Concrete object state.
4. Relationship, trust, concealment, betrayal, refusal, protection, misjudgment.
5. Organization pressure embodied by a person.
6. Records, reports, logs, systems, databases, notices, numbers, and screens.

Record-driven plot is allowed only as a supporting layer.

## Hard Ban For Chapter Climax

The main chapter turn, climax, or chapter hook must not rely primarily on:

- submitting a report;
- receiving a system prompt;
- a database changing status;
- a case number being generated;
- a workflow auto-escalation;
- a log entry;
- an archive unlocking;
- a red warning / popup / notification;
- an email / message that substitutes for physical action;
- electronic evidence that no person acts on.

If one appears, it must immediately cause a concrete person or object action:

- someone blocks a door;
- someone tries to take a child / witness / object away;
- someone refuses to sign;
- someone hides or destroys an object;
- someone changes testimony;
- someone arrives in person;
- someone grabs, seals, moves, covers, locks, removes, trades, or protects a physical item;
- a relationship or alliance visibly changes.

## Better Plot Carriers

Prefer:

- a doctor trying to take a child away;
- a worker trying to remove a damaged part;
- a witness refusing to speak unless protected;
- a superior arriving to silence the protagonist;
- a community elder blocking access;
- a security officer sealing a room;
- a black-market runner taking a physical object;
- a victim recognizing the protagonist;
- a colleague changing sides in front of others;
- a protagonist physically preserving, hiding, returning, swapping, or exposing an object.

## Evidence Priority

Prefer evidence that can be handled, hidden, damaged, lost, stolen, or used in a conversation:

- a bent handrail;
- a torn sleeve;
- a missing screw;
- a child's hairpin;
- a damaged phone;
- a witness's changed sentence;
- a door that should not be locked;
- a scar, bruise, burn, footprint, residue, smell, pressure mark;
- a person who saw something but is afraid to say it.

Electronic records are weaker unless they create immediate physical or relational consequences.

## Protagonist Advantage Rule

A protagonist advantage should be usable in the next scene.

Strong advantages:

- a person trusts the protagonist enough to let them enter;
- a witness gives the protagonist a name;
- a damaged object remains in the protagonist's reach;
- an opponent misjudges what the protagonist knows;
- a stronger actor cannot act without negotiating with the protagonist;
- the protagonist gains ten minutes before someone removes a person or object;
- a relationship shifts in public.

Weak advantages:

- the system changes status;
- a report is submitted;
- a number is generated;
- the protagonist knows `this is not simple`;
- an archive contains a clue but no one acts yet.

## Record Use Rule

Before using a system, report, log, file, screen, or database, answer:

1. Who sees it?
2. Who changes action because of it?
3. What object, route, person, or relationship changes immediately?
4. Can the same effect be carried by a person or object instead?
5. If this record is removed, does the scene still have conflict?

If the answer is unclear, remove the record or demote it to background.

## Checks

Mark `REVISE` if:

- the chapter's main tension is waiting for a system result;
- the protagonist's key choice is clicking, submitting, uploading, approving, logging, or filling a form;
- the climax is an automatic notification;
- organizations appear only as system labels rather than embodied people;
- evidence exists only as a digital record;
- no person physically acts on the information;
- the reader reward is only a status change;
- the ending is a screen prompt instead of a scene consequence.

## Repair Method

Convert record-driven beats into scene action.

Weak:

```text
The system marked the case as abnormal.
```

Stronger:

```text
The doctor stopped at the stairwell after one call, turned back to the child, and said the ambulance would not leave yet.
```

Weak:

```text
The report triggered an investigation.
```

Stronger:

```text
A man in plain clothes came up the stairs, showed his badge only to the doctor, and told the nurse to keep the child in the room.
```

Weak:

```text
The protagonist submitted evidence.
```

Stronger:

```text
The protagonist put the bent handrail behind the community desk before the worker could unscrew it.
```
