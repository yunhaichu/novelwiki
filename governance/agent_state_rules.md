# Agent State Rules

These rules govern character-agent simulation for fiction writing.

The goal is to make story movement emerge from characters acting under partial knowledge and local pressure.

## Core Principle

A character is not a plot function.

A character may only act from:

- what they know
- what they want
- what they fear
- what they misunderstand
- what they can physically do
- what their social position allows
- what the scene currently pressures them to do

## Private Knowledge Rule

Each character has private knowledge.

Do not let one character use another character's private knowledge unless:

- they were told;
- they observed it;
- they inferred it from visible behavior;
- their role gives them access to it.

If a character is guessing, mark it as a guess.

## No Outline Knowledge Rule

No character knows the outline.

Characters should not act to create:

- a hook
- a twist
- a clue
- a payoff
- a theme
- a chapter ending

They act to solve or avoid their current pressure.

## Pressure Rule

Each active character must have current pressure.

Pressure may be:

- physical danger
- hunger
- punishment
- status loss
- shame
- debt
- duty
- suspicion
- desire for reward
- need to hide information
- fear of being exposed

If a character has no pressure, they should probably not drive the scene.

## Action Support Rule

Every important action must have a support source.

Support source examples:

- visible object
- prior line of dialogue
- local rule
- physical condition
- remembered fact
- social rank
- material need
- misunderstanding
- another character's move

If the action has no support source, revise the simulation before prose drafting.

## Reaction Necessity Rule

A reaction should follow from the reacting character's interest, fear, rank, knowledge, or misunderstanding.

Do not accept reactions that exist only to reverse the protagonist or push the plot.

## State Update Rule

After each round, update:

```text
who knows what now
who suspects whom
who gained or lost leverage
who has fewer options
what object, route, record, promise, or threat changed state
what pressure now forces the next move
```

If no state changed, the round is probably weak.

## Output Discipline

Simulation output is not prose.

Do not polish the language.
Do not write theme sentences.
Do not explain what the scene means.
Do not force a chapter hook.

Only record actions, motives, reactions, and state changes.
