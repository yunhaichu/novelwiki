# Reality Logic Review

Use this checklist after reality-causal preflight, after emergent chapter design, and after drafting.

The purpose is to ensure the chapter's events, people, institutions, bodies, resources, and timing obey the internal logic of the genre world.

## Required Inputs

Read:

- genre mode contract;
- reality-causal preflight;
- base settings;
- selected reference settings;
- dramatic arena;
- protagonist growth track;
- chapter design;
- chapter draft if present.

## Hard Checks

### 1. Genre Reality Check

Does the event obey the genre's own reality?

Mark `REVISE` if the chapter imports a convenient process, object, institution, or ability that the genre mode does not support.

### 2. Normal Process Check

Would the proposed situation normally unfold this way?

Check:

- who appears first;
- who has authority;
- what each actor would naturally prioritize;
- what can be done immediately;
- what requires time, permission, cost, or preparation.

Mark `REVISE` if the scene skips normal first responders, obvious actions, or standard constraints without justification.

### 3. Motive / Authority / Information Check

For each key action, ask:

```text
Who has motive?
Who has authority?
Who has information?
Who can execute?
Who would resist?
```

Mark `REVISE` if any key action lacks one of these without explanation.

### 4. Timing Check

Does the time pressure make sense?

Mark `REVISE` if the chapter claims urgency but the underlying process would take days, weeks, or months, or if a time limit is impossible under body/resource/training conditions.

### 5. Cost / Survival Check

Can the protagonist and other actors survive or perform the required action under their current body, status, money, food, cultivation, stamina, access, and knowledge conditions?

Mark `REVISE` if the story stacks costs for atmosphere but gives no plausible survival path.

### 6. Institution Cost Logic Check

Does the institution's action match its incentives and costs?

Mark `REVISE` if an institution takes expensive, risky, illegal, or high-level action when a cheaper, ordinary, lower-risk action would naturally come first.

### 7. Role Knowledge Check

Does each role know only what their role would know?

Mark `REVISE` if a side character knows precise information without a channel.

### 8. Result Attractor Check

Does the chapter result follow naturally from the preflight and normal process?

Mark `REVISE` if the chapter result is correct for plot needs but not a natural consequence of the setup.

## Repair Standard

Do not patch logic errors with explanation alone.

Repair by changing the event, entry point, actor, object, timing, or cost until the scene would still make sense if the protagonist were not present.
