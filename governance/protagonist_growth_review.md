# Protagonist Growth Review

Use this checklist after emergent chapter design, after drafting, and before chapter state update.

The purpose is to verify that the protagonist grows along the approved growth track rather than developing randomly, skipping levels, or receiving unearned leverage.

## Required Inputs

Read:

- protagonist growth track;
- protagonist character wiki;
- character behavior model;
- chapter design;
- advantage / reward ledger;
- chapter draft;
- previous chapter state;
- relevant major conflict and dramatic arena files.

## Core Principle

```text
The larger trend is hard to resist.
The protagonist should not stop it directly.
The protagonist should survive pressure, learn, pay cost, gain usable leverage, and slowly expand action space.
```

## Hard Checks

### 1. Active Growth Stage

Which growth stage is active in this chapter?

Mark `REVISE` if the chapter does not identify an active growth stage.

### 2. Pressure-To-Weakness Fit

Does the chapter pressure test the protagonist's current weakness?

Mark `REVISE` if the pressure is unrelated to protagonist growth.

### 3. Cost Check

What cost does the protagonist pay?

Mark `REVISE` if the protagonist gains leverage without cost, exposure, debt, suspicion, loss, or limitation.

### 4. Usable Leverage Check

What usable leverage does the protagonist gain?

Valid leverage includes:

- relationship;
- physical object;
- witness;
- access;
- operational space;
- time;
- enemy misjudgment;
- skill / judgment upgrade;
- a specific information advantage that changes the next action.

Mark `REVISE` if the gain is only mood, vague insight, or abstract confidence.

### 5. No Jump Check

Does the protagonist skip a stage?

Mark `REVISE` if the protagonist moves from survival to control, from evidence to organization leverage, or from local influence to structural victory too early.

### 6. Large Trend Respect

Does the protagonist respect the current larger trend?

Mark `REVISE` if the protagonist directly defeats or neutralizes a major structural pressure too early.

### 7. Reuse Check

Can the chapter's growth be reused later?

Mark `REVISE` if the lesson cannot affect later choices.

### 8. Character Consistency Check

Does the growth fit the protagonist's behavior model, environmental modulation, threshold behavior, and aftermath?

Mark `REVISE` if the protagonist becomes a different person without a threshold trigger or aftermath.

### 9. Support Character Check

Do other characters retain their own motives?

Mark `REVISE` if support characters exist only to hand the protagonist a clue, object, trust, access, or upgrade.

### 10. State Update Check

Can the growth be written into chapter state as a concrete change?

Mark `REVISE` if nothing changes except the protagonist's feelings.

## Review Standard

A strong chapter does not merely move the plot.

It moves the protagonist one controlled step along the growth track.

The step should be small enough to be believable and useful enough to matter.
