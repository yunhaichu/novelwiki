# Reader Reward Engine

Use this during outline review, volume review, beat review, chapter brief review, and chapter review.

The goal is to ensure that a story does not only create pressure, but also pays the reader through desire compensation, emotional return, status movement, information gain, or anticipation.

## Core Idea

Genre fiction is a promise to the reader.

A chapter, beat, volume, or whole novel should identify:

```text
Reader desire activated:
Reader pressure or frustration:
Protagonist action:
Cost:
Visible reward:
Who confirms the reward:
Next desire opened:
```

## Desire Categories

A story may activate one or more of these:

```text
survival
status rise
resource gain
revenge / justice
competence
recognition
freedom
belonging
romance / intimacy
truth discovery
control over rules
moral vindication
security
wonder / exploration
fear / tension release
```

## Genre Promise Mapping

The reward must fit the main genre promise.

Examples:

```text
cultivation: power, resource, rule understanding, survival in sect/system, status movement
urban: money, reputation, relationship, career leverage, social/legal pressure
science fiction: anomaly proof, rule discovery, technical breakthrough, human cost
mystery: clue, contradiction, suspect relation, proof chain, fair reveal
romance: relationship movement, emotional risk, intimacy, commitment, repair
farming/management: resource chain, production, land, community, defense, market access
political/officialdom: position, alliance, procedure, policy risk, public reputation
horror/thriller: threat escalation, survival move, partial escape, deeper dread
```

## Reward Timing

A reward can be small, but it must be legible.

Valid rewards include:

- a position improves
- a resource is gained
- a rule becomes usable
- a false path is eliminated
- a relationship changes
- a watcher reacts
- an opponent is delayed or forced to adapt
- a clue changes the reader's understanding
- a prior loss becomes useful
- a future possibility opens clearly

## Visibility Rule

At least part of the reward should be visible on the page.

A hidden clue alone is usually too weak unless the chapter is intentionally suspense-only.

Useful visibility signals:

- another character reacts
- a task or position changes
- a choice becomes available
- a previously closed path opens
- an opponent changes behavior
- a record, mark, token, object, clue, or relationship state changes

## Debt and Repayment

Do not stack pressure indefinitely.

Track emotional debt:

```text
What did the reader endure through the protagonist?
How soon is part of it repaid?
Is the repayment proportional, partial, delayed, or deliberately denied?
What larger payoff is promised if repayment is delayed?
```

## Failure Cases

Mark REVISE if:

- the chapter only adds suffering
- the reward is private and invisible
- the reward does not connect to the main genre promise
- the protagonist gains status but no story progress
- pressure is delayed without a clear future payoff
- the scene pays a side promise while ignoring the main promise
- the reader desire activated at the beginning is not addressed by the end

## Output Block

When reviewing, include:

```text
Reader desire:
Main pressure:
Visible reward:
Genre promise relevance:
Reward confirmation:
Next desire opened:
Failure risk:
```
