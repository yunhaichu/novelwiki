# Reader Entry Gate: Chapter 001

## Chapter / opening unit

Chapter 001.

## Target reader

Readers who enjoy transmigration, Journey to the West derivative fiction, small-character survival, light comedy, and practical counterattack.

## Primary genre promise

Survival counterattack: a modern person wakes as a weak little demon on the Journey to the West route and must survive through caution, canon memory, and small rule advantages.

## First 500 words must make clear

1. The protagonist has become a small demon.
2. He is weak and low-ranking.
3. He is being assigned to patrol.
4. The patrol route may soon overlap with Tang Sanzang and Sun Wukong.
5. He cannot say what he knows openly.
6. His first survival choice creates suspicion from a superior demon.

## Reader should remember only these 3-4 things

1. He wakes as a little demon during roll call.
2. Patrol duty can get him killed.
3. He recognizes a Journey to the West route sign.
4. He tries to avoid the dangerous road without exposing himself.

## Information allowed now

- The little demon body.
- Cave roll call.
- Patrol token.
- A simple mountain/road sign that triggers his canon memory.
- Patrol Captain Hei Ya.
- Fellow little demon Mao Qi.

## Information delayed

- Full canon route.
- Full heavenly/buddhist hierarchy.
- Full cultivation system.
- Full cave power structure.
- Exact transmigration mechanism.
- Direct appearance of Sun Wukong.

## Terms forbidden in opening

No invented cultivation ranks.
No long list of demon titles.
No system interface.
No abstract terms like destiny, heavenly chessboard, ancient calamity, or karmic net.

## Worldbuilding limit

Only show that the cave has roll call, patrol duties, patrol tokens, and punishment for bad excuses.

## Backstory limit

Only show the protagonist was modern and remembers Journey to the West enough to fear the route. Do not summarize his whole previous life.

## Opening action / pressure

He wakes during roll call with claws, fur, and a patrol token, then hears he is assigned to inspect the road near White Bone Ridge.

## Opening image or object

A rough wooden patrol token stamped with the words:

```text
白骨岭西道
```

## Protagonist visible choice

He tries to swap patrol routes by claiming the west road has fresh monk scent, hoping stronger demons will take the dangerous route seriously.

## Visible cost or consequence

Hei Ya becomes suspicious and orders him to lead the first half of the patrol instead of avoiding it completely.

## End-of-chapter question

Can the protagonist survive the west road after his excuse backfires and makes him the lead scout?

## One-sentence reader recap target

A modern man wakes as a weak little demon, recognizes that his patrol route lies on a deadly Journey to the West path, and tries to dodge the assignment, only to be made lead scout.

## Hard failure risks

- lore overload: avoid by using only the patrol token and White Bone Ridge sign.
- passive canon-watching: avoid by forcing an immediate route-swap attempt.
- pure comedy: keep the punishment and patrol risk real.
- instant power fantasy: protagonist stays weak.
