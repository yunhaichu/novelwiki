# Project Outline: 西游小妖

## Project title

西游小妖

## Genre / story engine

Mythic transmigration / survival counterattack with light xianxia comedy.

## Primary reader promise

A modern person becomes a low-ranking little demon in the Journey to the West world and must survive by using canon memory, small-rule awareness, and practical choices rather than strength.

## One-sentence core conflict

The protagonist wakes as a nameless patrol demon in a cave that will soon be swept by Sun Wukong, but escaping or warning others both risk immediate punishment from stronger demons.

## Opening imbalance

He wakes during cave roll call with a wooden patrol token in his claws and realizes the mountain name, route sign, and demon chatter match a coming Journey to the West disaster.

## Protagonist external goal

Survive the first route-clearing event and avoid being sent to the front line when Tang Sanzang's group arrives.

## Protagonist inner pressure

He wants to use memory to survive but is afraid of being noticed as abnormal. His old human habit is explaining too much; in this body, explanation can get him eaten or drafted.

## Primary obstacle

He is weak, low-ranked, and trapped inside a demon hierarchy where strong demons punish cowardice and humans/monks kill monsters without asking rank.

## Failure cost

If he follows orders, he may be sent to patrol the road where Sun Wukong appears. If he refuses, the cave's stronger demons may punish him first.

## Three-layer suspense

- Immediate: can he avoid today's dangerous patrol assignment without exposing that he knows the future?
- Midterm: can he find a small position, rule, object, or relationship that keeps him off fatal routes?
- Longterm: can a nobody demon survive inside a mythic route where famous powers casually erase minor monsters?

## Five turning points

- Imbalance: the protagonist wakes as a small demon during roll call.
- First tool or method: he uses knowledge of road signs and a minor cave rule to swap patrol routes.
- Relationship reconfiguration: a stronger but dim patrol captain starts treating him as useful rather than disposable.
- Truth turn: canon memory is incomplete; some local events differ because small demons have their own hidden survival customs.
- Terminal choice: he must decide whether to save only himself or use his low-rank network to redirect a group of small demons away from disaster.

## Core relationship matrix

### Protagonist / Patrol Captain Hei Ya

- Surface relation: little demon and patrol captain.
- Hidden tension: Hei Ya needs disposable scouts; protagonist needs to avoid the deadly route.
- Expected change: Hei Ya begins by threatening him, then values his cautious road-reading.

### Protagonist / Little Demon Mao Qi

- Surface relation: fellow low-ranking demon.
- Hidden tension: Mao Qi wants to copy the protagonist's safer choices but may expose him.
- Expected change: comic alliance with risk of betrayal through stupidity.

### Protagonist / Cave Master

- Surface relation: distant boss and cannon-fodder subordinate.
- Hidden tension: the cave master wants tribute and patrol control; protagonist wants invisibility.
- Expected change: the cave master eventually notices abnormal survival results.

### Protagonist / Sun Wukong

- Surface relation: future lethal-scale threat.
- Hidden tension: protagonist knows the name but cannot say it openly.
- Expected change: first volume treats Sun Wukong as deadline and off-page pressure, not direct early opponent.

## Volume 1 goal

Survive the first patrol crisis, avoid being assigned to the doomed road, and gain a small but real survival position in the cave.

## Volume 1 end state

The protagonist is still weak, but no longer a random expendable patrol demon. He has a safer duty, one low-ranking ally, and a dangerous suspicion from a stronger demon.

## Volume 1 required beats

- Chapter 1: wake as a little demon, understand the patrol assignment, and make the first route-avoidance choice.
- Chapters 2-3: verify the Journey to the West route danger and create a small excuse to avoid the worst road.
- Chapters 4-6: the first method works but draws suspicion; a fellow little demon copies him badly.
- Chapters 7-10: the first known mythic pressure arrives; he survives by using low-level cave rules instead of fighting.

## Main genre promise

Survival counterattack through rule understanding, low-status leverage, small relationships, and comic practical choices.

## Main-system progress type

- safer assignment;
- minor object or token;
- cave rule loophole;
- low-rank relationship;
- knowledge of route timing;
- reduced chance of being used as disposable scout.

## Reader entry requirements

### What chapter 1 must show

- He is now a little demon.
- He is weak and low-ranked.
- A patrol assignment can kill him.
- He recognizes enough Journey to the West context to know a famous danger is coming.
- His first survival choice creates suspicion.

### What chapter 1 must delay

- Full canon timeline.
- Full cave hierarchy.
- Full cultivation system.
- Full explanation of transmigration.
- Direct meeting with Sun Wukong.

### What chapter 3 must prove

- Future memory helps, but cannot solve everything.
- Low-rank rules matter.
- Surviving once creates new attention and risk.

## Consistency risks

- Do not make the protagonist too strong early.
- Do not let canon knowledge become perfect omniscience.
- Do not overload the opening with famous names.
- Keep small-demon survival concrete.

## Type drift risks

- Lore explanation replacing survival pressure.
- Pure parody replacing stakes.
- Passive canon-watching replacing protagonist action.
- Instant cultivation or system-cheat drift.

## False promise risks

- Do not imply the protagonist will soon fight major mythic figures.
- Do not promise a system interface.
- Do not promise easy moral superiority over all demons.

## Forbidden drift

Do not write a famous-character tour. Every canon reference must create immediate survival risk, rule pressure, or status movement for the protagonist.

## Evaluation fields

- Hook strength: high if the patrol assignment and recognizable route danger appear immediately.
- Reader entry clarity: high if the reader understands little demon, patrol, danger, and first choice without lore.
- Goal clarity: high.
- Relationship tension: moderate; should rise through Hei Ya and Mao Qi.
- Main genre promise relevance: strong.
- Expansion capacity: strong if each survival move changes duty, route, token, or suspicion.
- Biggest risk: famous Journey to the West lore can overwhelm the small protagonist.
