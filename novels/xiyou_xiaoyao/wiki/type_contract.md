# Type Contract: 西游小妖

## Project title

西游小妖

## Primary genre

Mythic transmigration / survival counterattack.

## Secondary genre

Light xianxia comedy and Journey to the West derivative adventure.

## Primary reader promise

A modern person trapped as a low-ranking little demon in the Journey to the West world must survive the coming route-clearing disaster by using canon knowledge, small-demon practicality, and rule-aware choices.

## Main payoff type

```text
survival / status / rule understanding / comic reversal
```

## What reader expects by chapter 1

- The protagonist wakes as a small demon, not a hero.
- He quickly realizes this is the Journey to the West world.
- He is in immediate danger because his cave lies on the route of Tang Sanzang and Sun Wukong.
- His first survival move creates cost or suspicion.

## What reader expects by chapter 3

- The protagonist proves he cannot rely on brute strength.
- He finds a small rule, loophole, relationship, or job position that gives him a survival path.
- A stronger demon notices that he is acting differently.
- The coming Journey to the West event becomes a concrete deadline.

## What reader expects by volume 1 end

- The protagonist survives the first local disaster without becoming omnipotent.
- He gains a low but real position, object, route, or relationship that keeps him alive.
- He learns that canon knowledge is useful but incomplete.
- His attempt to avoid one danger creates a larger mythic-system pressure.

## Opening must show

- The protagonist is now a low-ranking little demon.
- His immediate job or duty is mundane and dangerous.
- A named or recognizable Journey to the West threat is near, but not lore-dumped.
- Survival requires a practical choice now.

## Opening must not hide

- He is weak.
- He is not in control of the cave.
- The big-name mythic figures are lethal-scale threats.
- The story is about survival from below, not instant power fantasy.

## Information that can be delayed

- Full geography of the Journey to the West route.
- Full heavenly/buddhist power hierarchy.
- Full identity of every cave demon.
- Full cultivation system.
- Full explanation of how transmigration happened.

## Forbidden false promises

- Do not promise instant system cheats.
- Do not promise that the protagonist can fight Sun Wukong early.
- Do not make the story pure parody with no survival pressure.
- Do not let canon references replace present action.

## If genre-blended, which genre is primary

Survival counterattack is primary.

## How secondary genre supports primary genre

Xianxia and Journey to the West elements provide rules, threats, social hierarchy, and comic mismatch. They should support survival and status movement, not replace it with lore display.

## How the ending should pay the primary promise

The first volume should pay by letting the protagonist survive a known mythic danger through a low-status, rule-aware method. The reward should be modest: a safer position, a useful relationship, a minor object, or a route away from immediate death.

## Type drift risks

1. Turning into lore explanation about Journey to the West.
2. Turning into random comedy where survival no longer matters.
3. Turning into instant power fantasy.
4. Turning into passive canon-watching where the protagonist only observes famous characters.

## One-sentence reader contract

A modern person wakes as a nameless little demon on the Journey to the West route and must survive before Sun Wukong arrives, using memory, caution, and small-demon tricks instead of power.
