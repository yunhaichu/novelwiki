# Opening Chapter Brief: Chapter 001

## Chapter number

001

## Opening function

Reader-entry chapter, body/status reveal, and first survival choice.

## Primary reader promise

Survival counterattack from the position of a weak little demon in the Journey to the West world.

## One-sentence reader recap target

A modern man wakes as a weak little demon, recognizes that his patrol route lies on a deadly Journey to the West path, and tries to dodge the assignment, only to be made lead scout.

## Protagonist entry

He wakes in a cave roll call, squatting among small demons, with claws, fur, and a wooden patrol token pressed into his hand.

## Immediate pressure

Patrol Captain Hei Ya is assigning road patrols. The protagonist receives a token marked White Bone Ridge West Road.

## Protagonist visible desire

He wants to avoid the patrol route without revealing that he knows the future story.

## Obstacle

Low-ranking demons cannot refuse patrol. Bad excuses are punished. Stronger demons are suspicious of cowardice.

## Cost if no action

He may be sent toward the route where Tang Sanzang's group and Sun Wukong will eventually pass.

## One active choice

He tries to redirect the route by claiming the west road has fresh monk scent.

## Visible consequence

The excuse does not free him. Hei Ya becomes suspicious and orders him to lead the first half of the patrol.

## One concrete memory point

A rough wooden patrol token stamped with `白骨岭西道`.

## Who notices or reacts

- Hei Ya notices his strange reaction.
- Mao Qi, a fellow little demon, is impressed and frightened.
- Other small demons laugh until Hei Ya changes the order.

## End hook

His route-avoidance attempt backfires. He must walk first on the very road he wanted to avoid.

## First 500 words must show

1. He is now a little demon.
2. He is in roll call and low status.
3. The patrol token and White Bone Ridge sign trigger his fear.

## Information allowed now

- Little demon body.
- Cave roll call.
- Patrol token.
- White Bone Ridge West Road.
- Hei Ya and Mao Qi.
- Basic danger of Sun Wukong's coming route, without direct appearance.

## Information withheld

- Full canon route.
- Full cave hierarchy.
- Full cultivation system.
- Full reason for transmigration.
- Direct Sun Wukong appearance.

## Backstory limit

Only use brief modern memory: he knows Journey to the West and knows small demons on famous routes usually do not end well.

## Worldbuilding limit

Only show roll call, patrol token, route assignment, and punishment risk.

## Terms forbidden in opening

No invented cultivation ranks, no system UI, no abstract destiny/karmic language.

## Declared primary genre promise

Survival counterattack.

## How this opening makes it visible

The protagonist is weak, assigned to a dangerous route, tries a small survival trick, and pays a cost.

## What type promise must not be falsely signaled

Do not signal that he can fight famous mythic figures soon.

## Secondary genre if any

Light xianxia comedy and Journey to the West derivative adventure.

## How secondary genre supports, not replaces, the primary promise

Comedy comes from mismatch between modern knowledge and little-demon reality. Canon lore creates survival deadlines.

## Why must the protagonist act now?

Roll call is assigning patrol routes. Once the token is confirmed, he must go.

## What does the protagonist protect first in this scene?

His life. He also tries to protect his abnormal knowledge from being exposed.

## Which flaw, fear, or pressure is triggered?

He wants to over-explain because he knows the danger, but over-explaining would expose him.

## What wrong or partial choice can this produce?

He makes up a monk-scent excuse without understanding cave patrol politics.

## What consequence does the choice create?

Hei Ya decides he is either sharp or cowardly and puts him at the front of the patrol.

## Anti-overload notes

Keep chapter 1 to roll call, token, route danger, failed excuse, lead-scout consequence.
