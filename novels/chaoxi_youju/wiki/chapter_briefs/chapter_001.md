## Beat Role

Introduce the protagonist, closing postal center, platform transfer pressure, first future-dated dead letter, and the immediate South Levee deadline.

## Main Engine

A sealed dead-letter tray produces a letter addressed to Lin Zhao with tomorrow's postmark.

## External Event Or Pressure

The old postal center is closing and platform audit cameras are active during the final transfer shift.

## Protagonist Goal

Finish the transfer quietly, keep control over the old sorting floor for one last night, and avoid giving platform auditors a reason to flag him.

## Protagonist Action

Lin Zhao notices a dead-letter tray that should already be sealed has shifted position. He opens it despite audit risk and finds the future-dated letter addressed to himself.

## Main Obstacle

Opening the tray can be treated as a violation. Qin Man may see enough to suspect Lin Zhao is hiding something. The letter itself is impossible to explain.

## State Change

Lin Zhao changes from closing-shift worker to named recipient of a future warning.

## Relationship Change

Qin Man sees Lin Zhao conceal something. Their coworker relationship gains suspicion before the reader knows whether Qin Man will help or protect himself.

## Information Change

The letter predicts a South Levee bus incident at 17:40 tomorrow.

## Resource / Status Change

Lin Zhao gains possession of the first future-dated letter but risks being flagged by the audit camera and platform transfer logs.

## Visible Gain

The protagonist has a concrete object and deadline, not just a strange mood.

## Cost Or Consequence

Lin Zhao must decide whether to report the letter, hide it, or verify it himself. The act of opening the tray may already be logged.

## Who Notices Or Acts

- Lin Zhao notices the shifted tray and opens it.
- Qin Man notices Lin Zhao acting differently but does not know the full content.
- The old sorting machine reacts at the end.

## Next Hook
