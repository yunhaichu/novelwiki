# Outline Review: 潮汐邮局

## Decision

PASS.

## Main Reason

The outline has a clear main engine: future letters predict urban disasters, but using them costs memory. The opening hook is immediate, the protagonist's short-term and long-term goals are visible, and the father case gives a durable midterm mystery.

## Scores

- Hook strength: 5/5
- Goal clarity: 4/5
- Relationship tension: 4/5
- Main genre promise relevance: 5/5
- Expansion capacity: 4/5
- Consistency risk: 3/5

## Required Checks

### Core Conflict

PASS.

The protagonist goal, opposing force, failure cost, and irreversible pressure are present.

### Opening Hook

PASS.

The first strong hook appears immediately: a future-dated dead letter predicts a bus disaster and names the protagonist.

### First Three Chapters

PASS with requirement.

The first three chapters must contain at least these state changes:

1. Lin Zhao receives and verifies the future-dated letter.
2. The warning creates external danger and institutional suspicion.
3. Lin Zhao learns the old sorting machine can read a stamp layer that the new platform cannot.

### Five Turning Points

PASS.

All five turning points alter goal, relationship, rule, or risk.

### Relationship Matrix

PASS.

The key relationships contain surface relation, hidden tension, and expected change.

### Three-Layer Suspense

PASS.

The immediate disaster, midterm father case, and long-term mechanism are separable.

### Main Genre Promise

PASS.

The outline defines progress for suspense, light science fiction, and urban pressure.

### Drift Risk

PASS with caution.

The most likely drift is episode-of-the-week disaster mail. The beat table must avoid giving each chapter a new letter as the default engine.

## Required Follow-Up

Before chapter drafting:

- Create Volume 1 plan.
- Create 1-10 chapter beat table.
- Create story bible.
- Create glossary to control terms such as tide letter, old sorting machine, memory cost, and old mail route.

## Failure Library Watch

Watch for:

- low-anchor terms around postal jargon
- chronological log structure
- repeated letter-disaster-payoff loop
- genre promise drift into workplace routine
- Song Zhiyao becoming a data-explanation machine
