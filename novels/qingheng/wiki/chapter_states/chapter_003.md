# Chapter 003 State

## Review Result

ALLOW after minor style revision.

## Chapter Summary

Zhou Ping tries to recover from the incomplete first qi-guiding lesson, but shoulder pain and unclear memory leave him confused. He meets Liu Shun, a one-mark probationer, and learns that surviving to the next month changes one's standing but does not automatically solve anything. Zhou Ping and Wang Man are assigned to chop special wood in the nearby woodshed. The work is close but physically damaging, and they still fail to gain a good position at the qi-guiding lesson. Zhou Ping hears more instruction than before but becomes more confused, while someone ahead of him appears to feel something. Liu Shun gives only a limited hint: avoid the woodshed, but Zhou Ping lacks the one-mark status needed to do so.

## Core Drive

Zhou Ping wants to understand enough of the qi-guiding method to make progress before the three-month probation ends.

## Current Trigger

Zhou Ping's first lesson was incomplete, and his wet-mark memory aid failed. He begins the next day still confused and physically strained.

## Core Obstacle

Work assignments continue to affect learning access. Closer work is not necessarily easier. The woodshed damages Zhou Ping's hands and shoulder, and his body pain interferes with sitting and following the qi-guiding posture.

## Local Progress

Zhou Ping gets slightly closer to the qi-guiding lesson than the previous day and hears more instruction.

## New Trouble

More instruction does not create clarity. Zhou Ping learns that his earlier focus on heat was unreliable. He also sees someone else appear to feel something during the lesson, while he feels nothing.

## Ending Hook

Liu Shun suggests avoiding the woodshed, but Zhou Ping lacks the one-mark token status that seems to give older probationers better work options. Zhou Ping's token remains unmarked.

## Confirmed Facts

- Zhou Ping's shoulder pain continues after the lower-spring water task.
- Wang Man also sleeps poorly and remains physically strained.
- Liu Shun is a one-mark probationer living in the East Shed.
- Liu Shun wakes early and already knows some work-assignment patterns.
- First-day newcomers commonly receive the lower-spring task.
- The order of work assignment reflects token-mark status.
- Two-mark and one-mark probationers are assigned before new probationers.
- Liu Shun is assigned to sweep the front courtyard, which is close to the servant yard.
- Zhou Ping and Wang Man are assigned to chop special prepared wood in the woodshed.
- Woodshed work is close but physically hard.
- Chopped wood must meet specific standards; failed pieces do not count.
- Zhou Ping and Wang Man fail to complete the midday quota and must make up the work.
- Zhou Ping develops blisters in his palm and worsens shoulder strain.
- Zhou Ping and Wang Man arrive slightly earlier to the qi-guiding lesson than the previous day, but still cannot stand in the front lines.
- Liu Shun stands ahead of Zhou Ping and Wang Man but does not yield his position.
- Zhao Steward explains that qi is not wind, water, or heat, and that beginners only borrow heat as a way to recognize it.
- Zhao Steward also explains that back straight is not chest thrust forward, tongue touches the upper palate as a bridge, and the hand should touch below the navel without pressing.
- Zhou Ping realizes some of his earlier interpretation was wrong.
- Zhou Ping feels nothing during the lesson.
- Another probationer ahead of him appears to feel something and is warned by Zhao Steward not to chase it.
- Liu Shun gives limited advice that Zhou Ping and Wang Man should avoid the woodshed, but indicates their unmarked tokens prevent that.

## Character State

### Zhou Ping

- Remains confused about qi-guiding.
- Recognizes that hearing more instruction can also create more confusion.
- Suffers shoulder pain, hand blisters, and posture difficulty.
- Does not achieve qi sense.
- Notices the relationship between token marks, work assignment, and lesson position.
- Still has an unmarked token.

### Wang Man

- Remains under pressure and physically strained.
- Does not function as a pure comic role.
- Attempts to follow the qi-guiding words but is silenced by older probationers' reactions.
- Notices that others may be progressing before them.

### Liu Shun

- One-mark probationer in the East Shed.
- Knows practical patterns from experience.
- Gives limited, transactional, incomplete information.
- Does not act as a full mentor.
- Uses his one-mark status to occupy a better position and receive better work than newcomers.

### Zhao Steward

- Continues to conduct brief qi-guiding lessons.
- Does not check whether every probationer understands.
- Gives corrective instruction but does not personalize it for Zhou Ping or Wang Man.

## Story Memory Points

- First-day lower-spring work and later woodshed work both block learning access in different ways.
- Token marks affect not only identity but also work assignment and learning position.
- The woodshed is close, but close does not mean easy.
- More instruction can make Zhou Ping more confused rather than more capable.
- Another probationer appears to feel something before Zhou Ping does.
- Zhou Ping's wood token remains smooth and unmarked.
