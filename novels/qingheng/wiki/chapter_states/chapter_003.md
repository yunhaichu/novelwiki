## Golden-Three Function

Chapter 3 serves: Being Noticed Is Not Pure Reward.

It expands the visible return from chapter 2, creates a new cost, shifts Zhou Ping and Wang Man's relationship, gives Zhou Ping a false response in practice, and keeps Zhao Steward's register marks as an unexplained but visible hook.

## Chapter Summary

After Zhou Ping's visible gain in chapter 2, Wang Man feels left behind and asks why Zhou Ping did not call him earlier. The next morning Zhou Ping tries to include Wang Man, but Wang Man hesitates over the dirty work and loses the chance. Zhao Steward assigns Zhou Ping to clean the water channel behind the kitchen wall, while Wang Man is sent to carry water from the mountain-side well again. Zhou Ping performs the dirtier work and receives a new task from the worker behind the kitchen: remember and deliver changed bucket counts. He brings the old copper pieces and the bucket numbers to Zhao Steward before the qi-guiding lesson, drawing visible attention. During the lesson Zhao Steward teaches about false body sensations. Zhou Ping feels heat but does not chase it, while another probationer does and is corrected. After the lesson Wang Man confronts Zhou Ping. Zhao Steward adds a short stroke beside Zhou Ping's name, making the register marks more visible but still unexplained.
