# Chapter 003 State

## Review Result

ALLOW.

## Chapter Summary

Li Cheng wants to survive the night after the fat steward's warning, but the midnight dragging returns and the servant wood tokens begin to move, so he ties his token to his wrist and uses his old shoe to interrupt the gray-white powder entering the room, resulting in the powder retreating and the fat steward telling him not to wash the shoe because it may be relevant again that night.

## Core Drive

Li Cheng wants to stay awake enough to survive the night without breaking the night rule or losing his servant token.

## Current Trigger

The fat steward warned Li Cheng after the paving task: "Do not sleep too deeply tonight."

## Core Obstacle

During the midnight bell sequence, the dragging sound returns, the servant wood tokens move, and gray-white powder enters the room from under the door.

## Local Progress

Li Cheng ties his wood token to his wrist before sleeping. During the night, he prevents the token from being pulled away and pushes an old shoe in front of the gray-white powder.

## New Trouble

The powder retreats from the shoe, but some of it remains on the shoe. In the morning, the fat steward tells Li Cheng not to wash the shoe because "it will not recognize you tonight" if washed.

## Ending Hook

Li Cheng's servant work now clearly extends into the night, but he still does not understand the rules.

## Confirmed Facts

- Li Cheng ties his servant wood token to his wrist with a thin hemp cord.
- The round-faced boy copies Li Cheng and secures his own wood token.
- The midnight bells sound again.
- A dragging sound stops outside the East Shed again.
- Li Cheng's wood token moves under his sleeve during the night.
- The round-faced boy's wood token also moves.
- Gray-white powder enters the room through the door gap.
- Li Cheng pushes one of his old shoes down from the bed to block the powder's path.
- The gray-white powder retreats from the shoe.
- Some gray-white powder remains on or near Li Cheng's old shoe by morning.
- The fat steward notices Li Cheng's shoe and tells him not to wash it.
- The fat steward says: "If you wash it, it will not recognize you tonight."

## Observations Not Yet Explained

- The cause of the moving wood tokens is unknown.
- The cause and nature of the dragging sound remain unknown.
- The nature of the gray-white powder is unknown.
- The reason the powder retreats from Li Cheng's old shoe is unknown.
- The meaning of the fat steward's instruction about the shoe is unknown.
- Whether the shoe is being marked, recognized, avoided, or used as a tool is unknown.

## Li Cheng's Inferences

- Li Cheng thinks the midnight disturbance, gray-white powder, and prior paving task may be connected, but he is not certain.
- Li Cheng thinks protecting the wood token matters.
- Li Cheng thinks the old shoe may have affected the powder, but he does not know why.
- Li Cheng suspects servant work now includes night survival, not only daytime tasks.

These are Li Cheng's inferences and should not be treated as objective canon until later confirmed.

## Character State

### Li Cheng

- Shows caution by tying the wood token to his wrist before the night begins.
- Does not understand the night disturbance.
- Does not break the rule by leaving the shed.
- Uses a simple object, his old shoe, to interrupt the powder's path.
- Still avoids claiming certainty about the rules.
- Now has an old shoe marked with gray-white powder that should not be washed.

### Round-faced boy

- Follows Li Cheng's cautious behavior by securing his own token.
- Remains fearful but observant.
- Reacts to the shoe and the fat steward's warning with nervous humor.
- Does not understand the night rules.

### Fat steward

- Appears in the morning after the disturbance.
- Notices Li Cheng's shoe.
- Gives a practical instruction without explaining the full rule.
- Continues to know more than the new servants but withholds explanation.
