## Review Result

ALLOW as golden-three chapter 2 after glossary rewrite.

## Golden-Three Function

Chapter 2 serves: Bad Work Becomes an Entrance.

It proves that the three-month pressure is real, that work assignment controls access to the short qi-guiding lesson, and that unwanted work can become a usable crack if Zhou Ping actively chooses it.

## Chapter Summary

On the first work-assignment morning, Zhou Ping observes that older probationers with marks on the backs of their gray wood tokens receive better or earlier work while new probationers wait to be assigned. Zhao Steward assigns Zhou Ping and Wang Man to carry water from the mountain-side well, but Zhou Ping steps forward and chooses the dirty bucket work behind the kitchen instead. Other new probationers laugh at him, but Zhao Steward changes Zhou Ping's assignment to washing buckets behind the kitchen while Wang Man and Liu Er are sent to the mountain-side well. Zhou Ping pays the cost through smell, dirt, broken skin, and being avoided, but he finishes earlier and reaches the qi-guiding lesson before Wang Man and Liu Er. He stands outside the fifth line with a wooden ladle from the kitchen-back area as proof of assignment, hears one complete key rule, and sees Zhao Steward add another dot beside his name.

## Reader Reward Ledger

- Reader expectation: Low-status unwanted work can become leverage.
- Obstacle: New probationers are passively assigned bad work, and bad work can cost access to the short qi-guiding lesson.
- Protagonist action: Zhou Ping volunteers for dirty bucket work because the kitchen-back area is closer to the lesson ground.
- Visible gain: Zhao Steward visibly changes Zhou Ping's assignment; Zhou Ping reaches the lesson early, stands outside the fifth line, and hears a complete instruction.
- Witness or reaction: Wang Man sees Zhou Ping separate from the mountain-side well assignment and later sees him standing ahead; Liu Er mocks Zhou Ping but is sent to the mountain-side well and later cannot speak; other probationers react to the smell and the wooden ladle; Zhao Steward adds another register dot.
- Next possibility: The dots in Zhao Steward's register and Zhou Ping's willingness to take unwanted work may bring further attention, but not a clean reward.

## Emotional Ledger

- Pressure: New probationers are expected to receive bad assignments and lose access to learning.
- Protagonist action: Zhou Ping actively chooses the dirty work others avoid.
- Cost: He becomes dirty, smells bad, breaks skin, is avoided, and creates distance from Wang Man.
- Visible compensation: He avoids the mountain-side well task, arrives earlier, stands closer, and hears one complete key rule.
- Who notices: Wang Man, Liu Er, other probationers, the kitchen-back worker, and Zhao Steward.
- New expectation: Zhou Ping can turn rejected work into access, but others may resent or exploit this.
