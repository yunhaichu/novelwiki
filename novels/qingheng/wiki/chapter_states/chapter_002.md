# Chapter 002 State

## Review Result

ALLOW after literacy-continuity revision.

## Chapter Summary

Zhou Ping and Wang Man move into the East Shed, learn that late arrival means missing the qi-guiding lesson, and are assigned to carry water from the lower spring. The distance and strict water measure cause them to return late, forcing them to stand at the back of the qi-guiding lesson. Zhou Ping only hears fragments of the method and uses wet marks on a bucket rim to remember them, but the marks dry quickly.

## Core Drive

Zhou Ping wants to keep access to the daily qi-guiding lesson and begin learning how to draw qi into the body.

## Current Trigger

Zhou Ping and Wang Man begin servant probation after entering Qingheng Sect.

## Core Obstacle

New probationers receive less favorable work assignments. The water-carrying task takes time and damages Zhou Ping's body before the qi-guiding lesson.

## Local Progress

Zhou Ping attends his first qi-guiding lesson, but only from the back and only after missing the beginning.

## New Trouble

Zhou Ping does not hear the full instruction. His shoulder pain makes sitting upright difficult. His memory aid is only wet marks on a bucket rim, and the marks fade quickly.

## Ending Hook

The first day proves that access to learning is not only about aptitude. Work assignment, time, body pain, position, and memory all affect whether a probationer can learn anything from the short lesson.

## Confirmed Facts

- Zhou Ping and Wang Man live in the East Shed.
- Some existing probationers or servants already have one or two marks on the back of their wood tokens.
- Zhao Steward announces that work assignment begins at mao hour.
- The qi-guiding lesson happens before evening class and is not repeated for latecomers.
- Work assignments are not equal.
- People with more marks on their tokens are assigned before newcomers and often receive nearer or lighter work.
- Newcomers stand last during assignment.
- Zhou Ping and Wang Man are assigned to carry water from the lower spring.
- Water must be delivered to a strict level; being short by half an inch requires redoing the task.
- The water-carrying task makes Zhou Ping and Wang Man late for the qi-guiding lesson.
- The qi-guiding lesson space has marked lines, and the front positions are limited.
- Zhou Ping and Wang Man stand at the back and miss the start of the lesson.
- Zhao Steward gives fragments of qi-guiding instruction, including: do not use the nose to inhale; keep the back straight; tongue touches the upper palate; hand stays below the navel; notice whether there is heat; do not try to move it.
- Zhou Ping hears only part of the instruction.
- Zhou Ping's shoulder pain makes it difficult to sit correctly.
- Zhou Ping uses wet marks on a bucket rim to remember fragments of the lesson.
- The wet marks dry quickly.

## Character State

### Zhou Ping

- Notices that work assignments affect access to learning.
- Does not fully understand the qi-guiding instruction.
- Misses part of the lesson and cannot reconstruct it completely.
- Does not solve the problem cleanly.
- Uses non-word marks rather than full writing to remember fragments, consistent with limited literacy.
- Pays a physical cost: shoulder pain from carrying water.

### Wang Man

- Shares the water-carrying assignment with Zhou Ping.
- Is physically strained by the work.
- Realizes that work may cause them to miss learning access.
- Does not become a pure joke role.
- Admits he did not hear the lesson fully.

### Zhao Steward

- Assigns work by token-mark status and newcomer status.
- Gives brief practical announcements.
- Conducts the qi-guiding lesson without checking who understood.
- Does not repeat missed instruction.

## Story Memory Points

- More token marks appear to correspond with better or earlier work assignment.
- Late work means a worse position at the qi-guiding lesson.
- The qi-guiding lesson is measured by a burning incense stick with a red line.
- Zhou Ping remembers the lesson with wet marks on a bucket rim instead of full written notes.
- The marks dry before he can preserve them.

## Open Questions

- Can Zhou Ping recover the missing parts of the qi-guiding method?
- How much do token marks affect work assignment and learning access?
- Can Zhou Ping find a way to return earlier or stand closer next time?
- What do older probationers know about improving access to the lesson?
- Will shoulder pain affect Zhou Ping's ability to practice the posture?

## Must Inherit Next Chapter

- Do not make Zhou Ping suddenly understand qi-guiding completely.
- Zhou Ping should still have only fragments of the first lesson.
- The connection between work assignment and learning access must remain important.
- Zhou Ping's shoulder pain may affect later practice.
- Wang Man should remain under pressure and should not become a pure comic role.
- Continue connecting servant work to cultivation access, resource distribution, and probation pressure.
