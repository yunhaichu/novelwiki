# Qingheng Golden Three Review

## Decision

PASS with follow-up constraints.

## Opening Diagnosis

The rewritten first three chapters now function as a proper opening unit rather than a slow process log.

Chapter 1 establishes low status, the three-month probation pressure, and Zhou Ping's small usable trait with account numbers.

Chapter 2 turns unwanted dirty work into visible access: Zhou Ping avoids the lower-spring assignment, reaches the qi-guiding lesson earlier, stands closer, hears a complete rule, and receives another register dot.

Chapter 3 expands that return into a double edge: Zhou Ping is used for dirtier work, gains visible usefulness by reporting counts, avoids chasing a false sensation, receives another register mark, and creates real tension with Wang Man.

## Strongest Hook

The strongest hook is not cultivation success. It is the register layer.

Zhou Ping's wood token is still blank, but Zhao Steward's register already distinguishes him from ordinary new probationers.

This creates a strong opening promise:

```text
Officially, Zhou Ping is still nothing.
Unofficially, the system has begun to mark him.
```

## Reader Reward Ledger

- Reader expectation: Ordinary low-status people can still find a crack in a system not built for them.
- Main obstacle: Thin aptitude, no offering, no status, short qi-guiding access, and passive work assignment.
- Protagonist's active pattern: Zhou Ping notices small administrative and spatial details, takes unwanted work, uses limited number literacy, and chooses not to chase false sensations.
- Visible gain by chapter 3: Zhao Steward repeatedly records him; he stands closer than other blank-token newcomers; he receives and reports count information; Liu Shun gives a longer practical warning.
- Who notices: Wang Man, Liu Er, Liu Shun, Zhao Steward, and nearby probationers.
- Larger possibility opened: There may be an unofficial evaluation path separate from monthly wood-token marks.
- Reason to continue: Readers want to know whether the register marks can become real advantage or a trap.

## Emotional Ledger

- Pressure: Zhou Ping is low-status, humiliated by aptitude testing, forced into servant probation, and pushed toward dirty work.
- Protagonist action: He uses observation, limited literacy, and unwanted work to create openings.
- Cost: Dirt, smell, injury, social avoidance, Wang Man's resentment, and risk of being labeled useful only for dirty work.
- Visible compensation: Earlier access, complete instruction fragments, register marks, and others' changed reactions.
- Who notices: Multiple named and unnamed characters notice the change.
- New expectation: Zhou Ping can climb through cracks, but each crack may cut him.

## Weakest Retention Risks

### 1. Dialogue may become too declarative

Some lines are clear and useful for fast-paced fiction, but future chapters should avoid turning emotional conflict into thesis statements.

Risk examples:

```text
I also want to stay, not only you.
```

This is acceptable once because it clarifies Wang Man's pressure, but future conflict should be shown through sharper practical lines, choices, and actions.

### 2. Register marks must not stay abstract too long

The register dots/stroke are an effective hook, but they must produce concrete consequences soon.

By the next 2-3 chapters, the story should show one of:

- a changed assignment
- a private warning
- a small privilege
- a new burden
- a ranking implication
- someone else asking about the marks

### 3. Dirty work cannot remain the only crack

Dirty work has served the opening well.

The next stage should broaden into trade, information, ranking, or practice error correction. Otherwise the story may again become a task cycle.

## Required Structural Follow-Ups

1. The next arc must convert register marks into visible consequences without fully explaining the system.

2. Wang Man's conflict must remain active. He should not reset into loyal sidekick or comic relief.

3. Zhou Ping must seek another kind of crack beyond dirty work: trade, memory, numbers, placement, silence, or timing.

4. The first real qi response should not happen immediately. Before that, Zhou Ping should gain one clearer distinction between false response and real response.

5. Liu Er should either create small friction or become evidence that other probationers now notice Zhou Ping's abnormal treatment.
