# Qingheng Golden Three Design

## Overall Goal

The first three chapters must make the reader feel clearly:

A boy with ordinary aptitude and no family resources is pressed to the bottom of Qingheng Sect, but he is not only enduring. He can turn things others dislike, ignore, or discard into cracks he can climb through.

By the end of chapter 3, the reader should expect:

```text
Zhou Ping is not a genius.
But he may be the kind of person who can dig a path out of bad rules.
```

## Core Reader Expectations

The opening should not promise instant invincibility.

It should answer these reader expectations:

```text
Ordinary people also want to rise.
Low-status people also want to be seen.
People without resources also want to find a crack.
What others reject can become my leverage.
I have no formal qualification, but I can make the rules remember me first.
```

## Golden Three Main Line

```text
Chapter 1: Entry Means Low Status
Chapter 2: Bad Work Becomes an Entrance
Chapter 3: Being Noticed Is Not Pure Reward
```

## Chapter 1: Entry Means Low Status

### Function

Establish protagonist, threshold, and three-month countdown.

Do not spend too long on line-waiting or ordinary testing flow.

The opening should show elimination pressure early.

### Opening Hook

At the sect gate, Zhou Ping should not first look at scenery.

He should first see a three-mark wood token being removed.

The text does not need to fully explain where that person went, but the reader should understand that three marks mean time is up, not honor.

### Core Conflict

Zhou Ping wants to enter Qingheng Sect.

Obstacles:

```text
thin aptitude
no family offering
limited literacy
only servant probation is available
must draw qi within three months
```

### Protagonist Action

Zhou Ping should not only be judged passively.

At entry, he should show one small usable trait:

```text
he recognizes account numbers
he notices registration order is not random
he notices wood-token marks may matter
he understands "probation" is not the same as "disciple"
```

This is not a cheat. It comes from lower-level life experience with grain, accounts, numbers, and allocation.

### Visible Return

When Zhou Ping says he knows some account numbers, Zhao Steward pauses, looks at him again, and adds a small note in the register.

The reader should see this return, but its meaning should not be fully explained.

### Ending

Zhou Ping receives the East Shed bamboo slip, and a splinter pierces his palm.

At the same time, Zhao Steward's register already has an extra note beside his name.

Ending feeling:

```text
He has been pressed to the bottom.
But he is not completely unseen.
```

## Chapter 2: Bad Work Becomes an Entrance

### Function

Prove immediately that the three-month pressure from chapter 1 is real.

Do not write ordinary servant life.

Chapter 2 must show:

```text
work assignment = cultivation access distribution
position = resource
time = resource
dirty or hard work = possible crack
```

### Core Conflict

New probationers are assigned bad work, causing them to miss or lose access to the qi-guiding lesson.

Do not write a long sequence of different daily jobs.

Compress routine labor and focus on unfair distribution and Zhou Ping discovering a crack.

### Protagonist Action

Zhou Ping discovers:

```text
waiting for assignment means being assigned passively
choosing unwanted work first may finish earlier
others reject dirty work, but dirty work is near the lesson area
```

He actively chooses an unwanted task, such as washing buckets, emptying dirty buckets, or cleaning the kitchen drain.

### Cost

The cost must be clear:

```text
smell
dirt
broken skin
being avoided
Wang Man does not understand
other new probationers mock him
```

### Visible Reader Reward

The payoff must be visible immediately.

Example:

```text
During assignment, Zhao Steward removes Zhou Ping from the lower-spring group and changes him to the kitchen-back area.
Wang Man sees it.
Other new probationers see it.
Someone who laughed at Zhou Ping for being dirty is sent to the lower spring.
```

This is the reward:

```text
Others think he has taken a loss.
He has actually exchanged it for access.
```

### Qi-Guiding Lesson

Zhou Ping stands closer than before and hears one complete key rule.

He must not successfully draw qi.

### Ending

Zhao Steward places a small dot beside Zhou Ping's name in the register.

Zhou Ping does not know what it means.

The reader should understand:

```text
The system has noticed him.
```

## Chapter 3: Being Noticed Is Not Pure Reward

### Function

Expand the small return from chapter 2 while creating a new problem.

Chapter 3 should show:

```text
being noticed is not purely good
if the sect learns Zhou Ping can endure unwanted work, it may continue using him that way
```

### Core Conflict

Zhou Ping wants to use the register dot for a better chance.

But what the sect gives him is not a clean reward. It is a more useful form of low-status exploitation.

### Protagonist Action

Zhou Ping should begin to choose, not only endure:

```text
continue taking dirty work for time
refuse to protect body and relationship
bring Wang Man or not
trade with Liu Shun for a more practical sentence
```

Wang Man must be involved, or Wang Man will become thin.

### Wang Man Relationship Shift

Wang Man should not only ask questions.

He should have real emotion:

```text
You went early by yourself?
Why did you not call me?
You stood in front while I stayed behind.
I also want to stay, not only you.
```

This is not just arguing. It is the first thorn in their relationship.

### Visible Reader Reward

Through the cost paid in chapters 1-2, Zhou Ping gains a clear small advantage:

```text
Zhao Steward lets him go somewhere early in front of others.
Liu Shun voluntarily says one more useful sentence.
A probationer who mocked him gets stuck behind.
Zhou Ping receives one key sentence that others did not hear fully.
```

There must be a visible reaction from another character.

### Cultivation Feedback

Give the first false response:

```text
Zhou Ping feels heat in his lower abdomen.
He thinks it may be progress.
Zhao Steward says it may be hunger, fatigue, or pain, not qi.
```

This is not failure only. It is useful progress:

```text
He has eliminated one wrong path.
```

### Ending

Do not use a polished summary line.

Possible ending:

```text
Zhou Ping lowers his head and looks at his wood token.
The back of the token is still unmarked.
But Zhao Steward's register already has two dots beside his name.
```

Ending feeling:

```text
He is still not a disciple.
But he is no longer exactly the same as other new probationers.
```

## Golden Three Rhythm

### Chapter 1

```text
low status confirmed + slightly noticed
```

### Chapter 2

```text
active crack-finding + dirty work becomes access
```

### Chapter 3

```text
small chance expands + relationship thorn + false response rules out a wrong path
```

## What The Reader Should Know After Three Chapters

```text
1. Qingheng Sect is not a fair school. It is a selection machine.
2. Three-month probation is real pressure.
3. The quarter-hour qi-guiding lesson is a scarce resource.
4. Work assignment controls cultivation access.
5. Zhou Ping is not a genius, but he can find cracks from low status.
6. Wang Man is not a comic sidekick. He also wants to stay.
7. Liu Shun is not a mentor. He is an older probationer.
8. Zhao Steward is not warm, but he observes.
9. Register dots are part of a hidden evaluation layer.
10. Zhou Ping's path is not aptitude-first. It is turning rejected things into leverage.
```

## Prohibited Moves

```text
1. The first three chapters must not become consecutive daily-task logs.
2. Zhou Ping must not only suffer without visible return.
3. Payoff must not be hidden too deeply.
4. Wang Man must not only ask questions.
5. Liu Shun must not become a mentor.
6. Zhao Steward must not explain too much.
7. Zhou Ping must not truly draw qi in chapter 3.
8. Cultivation must not drift into rule-horror.
9. Dialogue must not become literary riddles.
10. The reader must not finish feeling only tired.
```

## Golden Three Checklist

For each chapter, check:

```text
What reader expectation does this chapter activate?
What obstacle does Zhou Ping face?
What active choice does Zhou Ping make?
What cost does Zhou Ping pay?
What visible gain does Zhou Ping receive?
Who sees or reacts to that gain?
Does the chapter give the reader a breath of return?
Does the chapter open a stronger reason to continue?
```
