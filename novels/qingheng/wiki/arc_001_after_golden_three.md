# Arc 001 After Golden Three Roadmap

## Status

Golden three review result: PASS with follow-up constraints.

The story may proceed beyond the opening, but it must not return to ordinary daily-task progression.

After chapter 4:

- Zhou Ping is still an unmarked servant probationer.
- Zhou Ping has no true qi sense.
- Zhao Steward's register now contains multiple marks beside Zhou Ping's name.
- Zhao Steward has used Zhou Ping for dirty work, count delivery, and incense timing.
- Wang Man feels left behind but still wants to stay.
- Liu Er has noticed Zhou Ping's different treatment and may create friction.
- Liu Shun has given limited practical warnings but is not a mentor.
- Dirty work and timing work have helped Zhou Ping gain access, but these routes risk becoming labels or traps.

The next short arc must convert the opening hook into first-month pressure and cultivation relevance.

It must show that:

```text
Zhao Steward's register marks have consequences.

Zhou Ping must move beyond dirty work as his only path.
```

## Anti-Inertia Rule

Chapter 5 must not repeat this pattern:

```text
Zhou Ping receives or chooses a task.
Others mock him.
The task creates physical cost.
The evening lesson confirms a small gain.
```

Chapter 5 should begin from a different engine:

- an external notice
- a first-month rule
- a relationship choice
- a trade
- another character acting first
- a consequence from the incense timing
- a discovery that links earlier work to cultivation access

## Physical State Rule

Do not keep repeating the same hand wound, oil mud, dirty sleeve, or smell.

For chapter 5:

```text
Zhou Ping's hand wound is mostly scabbed.
His sleeve has been washed enough that smell is no longer a main detail.
If wound or smell appears, it must create a new consequence.
```

## Character Concealment Rule

Wang Man and Liu Er should not state all emotions directly.

Use behavior instead:

- Wang Man copies a method without admitting it.
- Wang Man speaks politely but acts too quickly.
- Wang Man helps for his own reason.
- Liu Er jokes instead of openly explaining resentment.
- Liu Er watches or imitates instead of only mocking.

## Cultivation Conversion Rule

The next useful gain must connect to cultivation itself.

Work-related access must convert into at least one of:

- clearer instruction
- better lesson timing
- understanding false sensations
- learning why line position matters
- discovering how incense, wind, posture, fatigue, or food affects qi-guiding
- first-month evaluation pressure

Do not let workplace status replace cultivation progress.

## Chapter 005 Target: Trade Instead of Dirt

Purpose:

Move beyond dirty work as the only crack and break the repeated assignment-payoff skeleton.

Reader expectation:

```text
Zhou Ping should use numbers, timing, memory, silence, or observation to get cultivation-relevant information.
```

Required elements:

- Chapter 5 must not begin with a new unpleasant task assignment.
- The chapter should begin with a consequence from chapter 4 or an external change.
- Zhou Ping uses limited number literacy, timing, memory, silence, or observation to exchange for something.
- The exchange must have cost or risk.
- Wang Man should be involved through action, not direct emotional explanation.
- Liu Shun may provide a fragment only if there is a reason: trade, debt, silence, or shared risk.
- The gain must connect directly to qi-guiding, false sensations, line position, incense timing, food/fatigue, or first-month evaluation.

Possible engines:

1. Wang Man tries to imitate Zhou Ping by moving early, but chooses wrongly. Zhou Ping can help or let him fail.
2. Liu Er repeats a rumor about the incense timing, creating pressure around Zhou Ping.
3. Liu Shun needs someone to remember a small count, and Zhou Ping trades that for a precise practice sentence.
4. Zhao Steward's accurate incense from chapter 4 causes someone to realize the lesson lasted slightly longer than usual.
5. A first-month notice appears, making wood-token marks urgent.

Possible payoff:

- Zhou Ping learns which line position is actually useful and which is only crowded.
- Zhou Ping learns one clear distinction between false heat and a useful first response.
- Zhou Ping learns that fatigue or hunger can ruin practice, turning work choice into cultivation strategy.
- Zhou Ping gains a small rule about incense or wind that affects hearing and sitting.

Possible cost:

- He owes Liu Shun or the kitchen worker.
- Wang Man sees him trade and copies badly.
- Liu Er learns that Zhou Ping's method can be imitated.
- Zhao Steward notices Zhou Ping asking or trading too much.
