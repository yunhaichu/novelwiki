# Arc 001 Outline

## Arc Title

Three-Month Probation.

## Arc Function

This arc must turn Zhou Ping from an unmarked servant probationer into someone who gains a narrow, unstable foothold in Qingheng Sect's cultivation system.

The arc should not be a chain of daily chores.

Every chapter should connect work, hierarchy, resource access, status, or relationships to cultivation progress.

## Core Promise

A low-status boy with ordinary aptitude finds small cracks in a sect system that was not built for people like him.

The reader should feel pressure, unfairness, and small satisfying returns.

## Main External Pressure

Zhou Ping has three months to draw qi into the body.

If he fails, he will be sent down the mountain.

## Main Internal Pressure

Zhou Ping is cautious and willing to endure, but he risks becoming someone who accepts any humiliation for a small chance.

He needs to learn not only how to endure, but also when to choose, trade, refuse, and take a visible risk.

## Reader Payoff Pattern

Do not rely only on suffering.

Use a cycle of:

1. pressure
2. attempt
3. cost
4. small payoff
5. new complication

Each chapter should contain at least one felt payoff, even if the overall situation remains hard.

Allowed payoffs include:

- Zhou Ping gains a small piece of useful information
- Zhou Ping avoids a worse assignment through cost
- Zhou Ping sees through a rule others miss
- Zhou Ping earns a small mark, notice, debt, favor, or resentment
- Zhou Ping beats a small unfair setup without becoming powerful
- Zhou Ping makes someone underestimate him or remember him
- Wang Man or another peer changes how they view him
- a hidden mechanism of Qingheng Sect becomes clearer

## Relationship Tracks

### Zhou Ping and Wang Man

Start as same-batch village boys.

Early arc should create distance: Zhou Ping starts taking ugly opportunities, Wang Man falls behind or feels left behind.

Do not make Wang Man a comic sidekick.

His pressure should become real: fear of being sent away, envy, shame, or dependence.

Possible beat: Wang Man resents Zhou Ping for finding small openings without him, then later needs Zhou Ping or helps him at cost.

### Zhou Ping and Liu Shun

Liu Shun is not a mentor.

He gives fragments because fragments cost little or because Zhou Ping has already paid some visible price.

Their relationship should remain transactional and guarded.

Possible beat: Liu Shun offers a more useful hint only after Zhou Ping accepts a cost or gives something in return.

### Zhou Ping and Zhao Steward

Zhao Steward watches more than he explains.

The small dots in the register may represent observed usefulness, obedience, initiative, or risk value, but do not confirm this too early.

Zhao Steward should not become warm.

## Near-Term Chapter Roadmap

### Chapter 005: The Register Dot

Purpose: turn the small dot into tension and reader payoff.

Possible content:

- Zhou Ping learns that the dot changes how Zhao Steward sees him, but not in a cleanly good way.
- He may be assigned to another unwanted but nearer job.
- Wang Man notices Zhou Ping's changed treatment and reacts.
- Payoff: Zhou Ping gets a slightly better listening position or a useful fragment because of his choice.
- Cost: smell, isolation, resentment, or being labeled as someone who will take dirty work.

Do not confirm the full meaning of the register dot yet.

### Chapter 006: First False Gain

Purpose: give Zhou Ping a plausible but mistaken sense of progress.

Possible content:

- Zhou Ping finally feels a sensation during practice.
- It may be pain, hunger, fatigue, or false heat rather than qi.
- Someone else laughs, warns him, or uses his mistake.
- Payoff: he learns one thing not to do.
- Cost: embarrassment, body strain, or lost position.

### Chapter 007: Small Trade

Purpose: introduce resource exchange among probationers.

Possible content:

- Zhou Ping trades labor, food, silence, or a written/number-related skill for a missing phrase or better timing.
- Wang Man may be involved, excluded, or hurt by the trade.
- Payoff: Zhou Ping gains a more complete instruction fragment.
- Cost: debt or relationship tension.

### Chapter 008: One Real Trace

Purpose: first real but tiny cultivation response.

Possible content:

- Zhou Ping experiences something that is not dramatic and may be easy to miss.
- It should not make him powerful.
- Payoff: the reader sees the first true step.
- Cost: he cannot reproduce it reliably.

### Chapter 009-012: Probation Ranking Pressure

Purpose: widen the arc beyond daily work.

Possible content:

- a small probation ranking, work reshuffle, inspection, or monthly mark approaches
- Zhou Ping's register dots or behavior affect placement
- Wang Man's situation worsens or diverges
- Liu Shun's one-mark advantage is challenged
- the first visible elimination threat appears

## Prohibited Drift

- Do not write endless daily chore chapters.
- Do not make each chapter only about a new job.
- Do not make Zhou Ping succeed because he is simply smarter.
- Do not make suffering the only reader experience.
- Do not let the story become quiet realism with no webnovel payoff.
- Do not make the first qi success too large.

## Required Next Step

Before writing Chapter 005, decide which arc beat it serves and what the reader payoff is.
