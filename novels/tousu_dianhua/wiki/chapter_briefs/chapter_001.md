# Opening Chapter Brief: Chapter 001

## Chapter number

001

## Opening function

Reader-entry chapter and first altered choice.

## Primary reader promise

Urban rebirth / workplace counterattack: Chen Jiming uses future memory and procedural knowledge to change a past failure while risking his current job security.

## One-sentence reader recap target

A reborn customer-service worker risks his transfer bonus for his mother's surgery by escalating the complaint call he once mishandled, and the caller gives him a clue from the old timeline that points above his level.

## Protagonist entry

Chen Jiming wakes into a quality-inspection shift at the customer-service outsourcing company, seconds before Liang Guilan's complaint call enters his headset.

## Immediate pressure

The call is being monitored. A clean shift helps him pass transfer-to-permanent review and receive money needed for his mother's surgery deposit.

## Protagonist visible desire

He wants to keep his transfer chance and prevent the old disaster from repeating.

## Obstacle

The system recommends transferring the complaint to the property-management platform, and quality inspection penalizes unauthorized promises or escalation.

## Cost if no action

If he follows the old procedure, Liang Guilan's warning may again be buried, and the later elevator incident can repeat.

## One active choice

He uses a neglected emergency-risk clause to record the call as a possible personal-safety issue instead of transferring it away.

## Visible consequence

Zhao Shuhe notices the violation risk and warns that the transfer form may not be signed.

## One concrete memory point

The screen displays Liang Guilan's complaint and the system recommendation to transfer the call away.

## Who notices or reacts

- Liang Guilan pauses instead of hanging up.
- Zhao Shuhe steps behind Chen's station.
- Luo Qing looks over from the next workstation.

## End hook

Liang Guilan says this is not the first problem with that elevator; someone was carried out last month. Chen checks the old record and sees a handler name connected to the future executive who once blamed him.

## First 500 words must show

1. Chen is back before the decisive call.
2. He needs the clean inspection for transfer and surgery money.
3. The old procedure and the safer action conflict immediately.

## Information allowed now

- Chen's rebirth awareness.
- The call screen.
- Mother's surgery deposit.
- Quality inspection.
- Emergency-risk escalation clause.

## Information withheld

- Full future hearing.
- Full executive chain.
- Full accident chain.
- Company acquisition background.

## Backstory limit

Only flash the previous outcome through short concrete fragments: transfer, next-day incident, later blame, mother surgery money lost. No full life summary.

## Worldbuilding limit

Only explain enough customer-service procedure to understand transfer vs escalation.

## Terms forbidden in opening

No abstract labels such as fate, old timeline, hidden chain, conspiracy, or rebirth system.

## Declared primary genre promise

Urban rebirth / workplace counterattack.

## How this opening makes it visible

Chen returns to an earlier work moment and makes a different procedural choice that risks his job but creates leverage.

## What type promise must not be falsely signaled

Do not signal easy wealth, pure detective fiction, or omniscient rebirth.

## Secondary genre if any

Light realistic suspense.

## How secondary genre supports, not replaces, the primary promise

The old complaint record becomes proof and leverage for workplace/status reversal.

## Why must the protagonist act now?

The call is live. If transferred away, the old path repeats and the caller may hang up.

## What does the protagonist protect first in this scene?

He first tries to protect both the caller's safety and his job review, but the scene forces him to choose risk over clean compliance.

## Which flaw, fear, or pressure is triggered?

His old habit of self-protective compliance is triggered by the quality inspection and transfer review.

## What wrong or partial choice can this produce?

He may over-rely on procedural wording instead of telling anyone the full danger.

## What consequence does the choice create?

He creates a formal record but becomes visible to his supervisor and nearby coworker.

## Anti-overload notes

Keep the first chapter to one call, one rule conflict, one clue, and one supervisor reaction.
