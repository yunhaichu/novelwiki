# Project Outline: 投诉电话

## Project title

投诉电话

## Genre / story engine

Urban rebirth / workplace counterattack with light realistic suspense.

## Primary reader promise

Chen Jiming uses future memory, customer-service rules, and procedural leverage to reverse the career and family ruin caused by a phone call he mishandled in his previous life.

## One-sentence core conflict

Chen Jiming returns to the day of a complaint call that once ruined him, but saving the caller means violating the workflow that he needs to pass in order to earn his mother's surgery deposit.

## Opening imbalance

He wakes during a live quality-inspection shift, minutes before the same call he once transferred away according to procedure.

## Protagonist external goal

Prevent the first elevator incident from repeating while keeping his transfer-to-permanent chance and medical-money path alive.

## Protagonist inner pressure

He wants to stop being the kind of person who survives by passing risk down the line, but he is still afraid of becoming the scapegoat again.

## Primary obstacle

The outsourcing company's workflow penalizes unauthorized escalation. His supervisors need clean quality-inspection records more than real risk handling.

## Failure cost

If he follows procedure, a death chain may repeat and he remains the future scapegoat. If he violates procedure openly, he may immediately lose his job and his mother's surgery deposit.

## Three-layer suspense

- Immediate: whether Chen Jiming can handle Liang Guilan's complaint without failing quality inspection.
- Midterm: why previous elevator complaints were routed away or buried.
- Longterm: how the complaint chain links to the future executive who accused Chen Jiming at a hearing.

## Five turning points

- Imbalance: Chen Jiming returns to the moment before Liang Guilan's call.
- First tool or method: he uses a neglected emergency-risk clause inside the customer-service handbook to force a formal record.
- Relationship reconfiguration: a coworker first warns him, then realizes his abnormal handling may protect the whole team.
- Truth turn: the elevator complaint chain was not simple negligence; old records were filtered to protect a service-platform acquisition.
- Terminal choice: Chen Jiming must choose between becoming safely promoted inside the system or exposing a chain that can still destroy his new position.

## Core relationship matrix

### Chen Jiming / Liang Guilan

- Surface relation: customer-service worker and caller.
- Hidden tension: in the previous life he transferred her away and never heard her full warning.
- Expected change: she becomes the first person whose trust gives him evidence beyond future memory.

### Chen Jiming / Zhao Shuhe

- Surface relation: team lead and subordinate.
- Hidden tension: Zhao needs clean inspection numbers; Chen needs procedural flexibility.
- Expected change: Zhao first suppresses him, then worries that ignoring him may create a bigger team risk.

### Chen Jiming / Luo Qing

- Surface relation: coworker at nearby workstation.
- Hidden tension: she wants stability and dislikes Chen breaking rules during inspection.
- Expected change: she becomes an unwilling witness to his first deviation.

### Chen Jiming / Feng Yao

- Surface relation: future company executive, currently unseen senior operations figure.
- Hidden tension: in the future Feng Yao blames Chen for a chain of failures.
- Expected change: first volume links Feng to earlier hidden handling records.

### Chen Jiming / his mother

- Surface relation: son and ill mother.
- Hidden tension: he uses work compliance to protect her, but the same compliance once helped ruin them.
- Expected change: her medical-money pressure makes his workplace choices morally and materially costly.

## Volume 1 goal

Alter the first elevator incident, preserve enough job leverage to avoid immediate collapse, and uncover that the complaint record chain was manipulated before the public incident.

## Volume 1 end state

Chen Jiming is no longer only a low-level customer-service worker. He has evidence that the complaint chain was mishandled above his level, but he has also become visible to people who benefit from the buried records.

## Volume 1 required beats

- Chapter 1: The complaint call arrives and Chen makes the first altered choice.
- Chapters 2-3: He verifies that the old complaint existed and that the record path differs from what he remembers.
- Chapters 4-6: His workplace risk rises; Zhao Shuhe and Luo Qing react differently to his abnormal handling.
- Chapters 7-10: The first incident is altered, but a higher-level record points toward Feng Yao.

## Main genre promise

Urban rebirth progress through status, leverage, procedural advantage, risk control, justice, and family survival.

## Main-system progress type

- professional leverage;
- hidden record access;
- workplace status shift;
- public-risk proof;
- medical-money pressure management;
- social responsibility chain exposure.

## Reader entry requirements

### What chapter 1 must show

- Chen Jiming is reborn before a decisive call.
- He needs transfer-to-permanent money for his mother's surgery deposit.
- The call was mishandled in his previous life.
- Procedure and rescue now conflict.
- His first altered action creates a visible risk.

### What chapter 1 must delay

- Full future hearing details.
- Full company conspiracy.
- Full executive role of Feng Yao.
- Detailed acquisition background.
- Complete accident chain.

### What chapter 3 must prove

- The complaint is not isolated.
- Chen's altered handling opens a different information path.
- His low position is both a weakness and a source of access.

## Consistency risks

- Future memory must not become perfect omniscience.
- Workplace procedure must remain understandable.
- Chen's first actions must stay within plausible customer-service authority.
- Mother's medical pressure must remain concrete, not sentimental decoration.

## Type drift risks

- Turning into generic office process.
- Turning into pure conspiracy mystery.
- Turning into easy wealth fantasy.
- Turning into moral lecture without status progress.

## False promise risks

- Do not signal stock/real estate/speculation rebirth.
- Do not promise pure detective investigation.
- Do not frame Chen as instantly powerful.

## Forbidden drift

Do not let the novel become a chain of complaint cases. Each case must change Chen's position, leverage, risk, proof chain, or relationship map.

## Evaluation fields

- Hook strength: high if the phone call and surgery deposit pressure appear immediately.
- Reader entry clarity: high if the first scene explains call, choice, and cost without company lore.
- Goal clarity: high.
- Relationship tension: moderate at start, should rise through Zhao and Luo.
- Main genre promise relevance: strong.
- Expansion capacity: strong if each complaint leads to career leverage and hidden record access.
- Biggest risk: workplace procedure becomes dull unless every process creates risk, leverage, or cost.
