# Base Settings

Novel ID: `cyberpunk_reclaim_line`

Canon status: initial setup.

## Genre Foundation

Cyberpunk future city, lower-city labor perspective.

The story should not rely on abstract neon, cool hackers, or omnipotent code. The city should appear through rent, medicine, body hardware, workstations, blocked doors, unpaid subscriptions, and physical object handling.

## City Model

City: 海岬市.

海岬市 is a coastal corporate city divided by elevation, transport permissions, medical access, and credit score.

北环下层 is not lawless. It is over-regulated in cheap ways and under-protected in expensive ways.

People at the bottom are not outside the system. They are trapped inside weak versions of it.

## Core Operating Logic

Bodies are rented, repaired, repossessed, recycled, and scored.

Implants are not personal property for most low-level workers. Many are leased through medical subscriptions, employment bundles, debt clinics, or repair contracts.

Returned implants are both:

- material inventory;
- legal and data liability.

A device must pass erasure before it can be stripped, refurbished, resold, melted, or logged as disposal.

## Identity / Authority Rules

People have legal names, work IDs, and credit identities.

Workstations read work IDs more often than legal names.

Low-level workers can touch returned hardware only inside assigned stations.

They cannot:

- access full medical records;
- bypass erasure logs;
- certify a device clean;
- remove inventory from the line;
- contact the original owner through official channels.

They can:

- pause a conveyor bay briefly;
- mark a piece as jammed, cracked, contaminated, or misrouted if physically plausible;
- move items within their assigned tray range;
- exploit small timing gaps between scanner, sorter, and supervisor checks.

## Organization Rules

### 中安医疗资产

Owns subscription-linked medical devices and returned body hardware.

Public language:

- patient safety;
- asset recovery;
- compliant erasure;
- affordable care.

Real interests:

- preserve recoverable hardware value;
- avoid data-liability scandals;
- maintain subscription dependence;
- punish low-level contamination without exposing upstream failure.

### 海岬城市信用局

Maintains credit access for housing, transport, medication, employment eligibility, and food subsidy.

It rarely appears as people in low-level scenes. It appears through blocked doors, inactive cards, transport refusals, and subscription denials.

### 北环再处理站

Processes returned medical hardware.

It is not a secret black site. It is a legal low-status facility where errors are pushed downward.

## Recycling Line Function

三号回收线 exists to turn returned implants into classified outputs.

Returned implant flow:

```text
intake crate
-> visible damage check
-> ID scan
-> erasure verification
-> material sort
-> refurbish / strip / melt / quarantine / legal hold
```

A low-level worker's station handles physical separation after partial machine checks.

Categories:

- clean shell: casing can be stripped or melted;
- refurb core: still valuable, sent to repair contractor;
- wet return: biological contamination, sealed tray;
- dead board: electronics scrap;
- locked memory: failed erasure, legal hold;
- misread object: scanner cannot classify, supervisor must clear.

Sorting has economic function:

- clean shells recover metals;
- refurb cores are resold into low-tier subscription packages;
- dead boards are shredded for trace metals;
- wet returns create biohazard cost;
- locked memory creates legal liability;
- misread objects delay the line and hurt worker metrics.

## Protagonist Ability Boundary

马宽 has an obsolete analog hearing implant from childhood.

The implant is not a superpower and not a hacker interface.

It can sometimes hear low-frequency offline handshake residue from nearby devices after failed erasure.

At opening stage it can only:

- notice a pulse or rhythm from a device within close physical range;
- distinguish dead silence from weak residual handshake;
- cause headache, tinnitus, nausea, or balance loss if overused.

It cannot:

- read files;
- decode speech;
- identify owners;
- hack systems;
- open locks;
- control devices;
- defeat corporate security;
- prove anything by itself.

## Resource / Body Cost Rules

Useful resources:

- minutes before a scanner check;
- access to a tray;
- work gloves;
- clean label stickers;
- supervisor inattention;
- coworker debt;
- inactive hardware shard;
- medication subscription time.

Costs:

- credit penalty;
- shift metric loss;
- job suspension;
- tinnitus;
- dizziness;
- sister's medication delay;
- legal contamination flag;
- coworker suspicion.

## Opening Location Rules

Opening should stay in:

- 北环再处理站;
- 三号回收线;
- worker locker aisle;
- lower-city medication kiosk;
- tram checkpoint if needed.

Do not start in corporate executive rooms, high-security labs, hacker dens, or city-wide conspiracy spaces.

## Social Behavior Rules

Low-level workers are practical, tired, and watched.

They may help each other, but usually through trades, shift coverage, silence, shared tools, or debt.

Supervisors are not automatically villains. They are pressured by throughput, contamination risk, and audit metrics.

Corporations should act through ordinary procedures and embodied agents before overt violence.

## Opening Chapter Constraints

Chapter 1 may show:

- 马宽 on 三号回收线;
- returned implants being sorted for concrete economic reasons;
- a weak offline pulse from a supposedly erased implant;
- his sister's medication subscription pressure;
- a small choice to delay or misroute one item.

Chapter 1 must not show:

- system hacking;
- AI awakening;
- decoding the pulse into full speech;
- corporate assassins;
- elite hacker ally;
- city-wide conspiracy exposition.

## Forbidden Setting Moves

- No protagonist instant-hacks a corporate system.
- No magical backdoor access.
- No data ghost speaking in complete sentences at the start.
- No clean fight scene on the recycling floor.
- No exposition dump about the whole city.
- No protagonist name or new named character without Name Gate.
