# Reader Entry Gate

Novel ID: `lingdian_huizhi`

Chapter / opening unit: Chapter 001

Target reader: modern urban suspense reader who expects a clear protagonist, immediate pressure, ordinary-world plausibility, and one concrete abnormal hook.

Primary genre promise: modern urban suspense with light anomaly. The opening must show an ordinary service process becoming unsafe, not explain a full supernatural system.

## First 500 Words Must Make Clear

1. Who is the protagonist?

Chen Lu, an outsourced night-shift customer-service worker.

2. What immediate trouble is present?

A customer-service ticket appears in his assigned queue with a submission time after the coming midnight, while the current time is still before midnight.

3. What does the protagonist want right now?

He wants to finish the last tickets of the night without another quality-score warning, because another abnormal record may cost his night-shift allowance.

4. What can be lost soon?

If he mishandles the future-dated ticket, the audit trail may point to him. If he ignores it, the reported locker injury may happen and leave him with an earlier ignored warning in his queue.

5. What is strange / unfair / dangerous / desirable?

The ticket describes a refund complaint for a parcel locker injury that has not happened yet. The system treats it as a normal assigned ticket and gives Chen Lu no safe category for handling it.

6. Why should the reader continue?

Chen Lu must decide whether to create a process-valid reason to act before the timestamp becomes real, while the only record that can help him may also expose him.

## Reader Should Remember Only These 3-4 Things

1. Chen Lu has weak service-process access, not real platform power.
2. A ticket from after midnight appears before midnight.
3. The warning is incomplete but points to a specific real-world risk.
4. Acting early creates audit suspicion; ignoring it may allow harm and blame.

## Reader Itch

What specific question should the reader want answered next?

Can Chen Lu stop the parcel-locker injury using only a defensible service record before the audit trail turns against him?

Why is this question tied to the protagonist's pressure rather than only to setting curiosity?

Because Chen Lu's job, night allowance, and blame record are directly at risk. The question is not only where the ticket came from; it is whether he can act inside a process that gives him no clean option.

## Information Allowed Now

- Chen Lu's role and limited access.
- The night-shift queue and quality-audit pressure.
- His practical need to avoid another quality-score warning.
- One future-dated ticket.
- Ticket ID, timestamp, complaint category, and one concrete risk fragment.
- One narrow action Chen Lu can take through normal process.

## Information Delayed

- source of future-dated tickets.
- whether the platform knows about the anomaly.
- whether records can change after midnight.
- long-term cost or mechanism.
- full corporate power structure.
- full personal backstory.

## Terms Forbidden In Opening

Avoid introducing abstract or unexplained terms such as:

- 零点回执 as a mechanism label before the scene earns it.
- 未来工单 as a system term before Chen Lu has reason to name it.
- 平台深层系统.
- 命运.
- 规则背面.

Use ordinary service language first: 工单、时间、回拨、备注、质检、升级、退款、柜门、取件码.

## Worldbuilding Limit

Only show the customer-service floor, assigned ticket queue, quality review pressure, Chen Lu's immediate allowance risk, and one abnormal ticket.

Do not explain the full platform architecture.

## Backstory Limit

One sentence maximum about Chen Lu's current quality warning and allowance pressure.

Do not open with family history, career history, or platform history.

## Opening Action / Pressure

Chen Lu is trying to clear his queue before shift lock. A new ticket enters his current queue with a submission time later than the current time.

## Opening Image Or Object

The service system clock and the ticket submission timestamp disagree.

## End-Of-Chapter Question

Can Chen Lu create enough process reason to prevent the parcel-locker accident without making the audit trail point back at him?

## One-Sentence Reader Recap Target

A night-shift customer-service worker receives a complaint ticket from after midnight and must act on its warning without admitting he saw the future.

None.
