# Reader Entry Gate: Chapter 001

Novel ID: `lingdian_huizhi`

Chapter / opening unit: Chapter 001

Target reader: modern urban suspense reader who expects a clear protagonist, immediate pressure, ordinary-world plausibility, and one concrete abnormal hook.

Primary genre promise: modern urban suspense with light anomaly. The opening must show an ordinary process becoming unsafe, not explain a full supernatural system.

## First 500 Words Must Make Clear

1. Who is the protagonist?

Chen Lu, an outsourced night-shift customer-service worker.

2. What immediate trouble is present?

A customer-service ticket appears in his assigned queue with a submission time after the coming midnight, but the current time is still before midnight.

3. What does the protagonist want right now?

He wants to clear the night queue without quality deduction or escalation blame.

4. What can be lost soon?

If he mishandles the ticket, he may leave an audit trail that makes him responsible. If he ignores it, the ticket's warning may become a real loss tied to his record.

5. What is strange / unfair / dangerous / desirable?

The ticket describes a refund complaint for a parcel locker accident that has not happened yet. The system treats it as an ordinary ticket assigned to him.

6. Why should reader continue?

Chen Lu must decide whether to create a defensible service record before the timestamp becomes real, while not exposing that he saw a future-dated complaint.

## Reader Should Remember Only These 3-4 Things

1. Chen Lu has weak service-process access, not real platform power.
2. A ticket from after midnight appears before midnight.
3. The warning is incomplete but points to a real-world risk.
4. Acting early creates audit suspicion; ignoring it may allow harm.

## Information Allowed Now

- Chen Lu's role and limited access.
- The night-shift queue and quality-audit pressure.
- One future-dated ticket.
- Ticket ID, timestamp, complaint category, and one concrete risk fragment.
- A narrow action Chen Lu can take through normal process.

## Information Delayed

- source of future-dated tickets.
- whether the platform knows about the anomaly.
- whether records can change after midnight.
- long-term cost or mechanism.
- full corporate power structure.
- full personal backstory.

## Terms Forbidden In Opening

Avoid introducing abstract or unexplained terms such as:

- 零点回执 as a mechanism label before the scene earns it.
- 未来工单 as a system term before Chen Lu has reason to name it.
- 平台深层系统.
- 命运.
- 规则背面.

Use ordinary service language first: 工单、时间、回拨、备注、质检、升级、退款、柜门、取件码.

## Worldbuilding Limit

Only show the customer-service floor, assigned ticket queue, quality review pressure, and one abnormal ticket.

Do not explain the full platform architecture.

## Backstory Limit

One sentence maximum about why Chen Lu fears audit blame if needed.

Do not open with family history, career history, or platform history.

## Opening Action / Pressure

Chen Lu is trying to close his queue before shift end. A new ticket jumps into his queue with a submission time later than the current time.

## Opening Image Or Object

The service system clock and the ticket submission timestamp disagree.

## End-Of-Chapter Question

Can Chen Lu create enough process reason to prevent the parcel-locker accident without making the audit trail point back at him?

## One-Sentence Reader Recap Target

A night-shift customer-service worker receives a complaint ticket from after midnight and must act on its warning without admitting he saw the future.

## Gate Decision

ALLOW for opening brief.
