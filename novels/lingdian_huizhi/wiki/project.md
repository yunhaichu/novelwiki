# Project

Novel ID: `lingdian_huizhi`

Working title: 零点回执

## Test Role

This is a test novel instance. It is used to validate the workflow, not to define global rules.

## Primary Genre

Modern urban suspense with a light anomaly mechanism.

## Reader Promise

A low-authority protagonist discovers that ordinary service tickets may contain warnings from shortly after midnight. The reader follows how he turns limited customer-service access, timestamps, callbacks, and process records into small but concrete leverage while avoiding audit and platform suspicion.

## One-Sentence Core

A night-shift outsourced customer-service worker starts receiving refund tickets submitted from the next day and must use weak process access to prevent real losses without exposing why he knows them.

## Protagonist Starting State

Chen Lu is a night-shift outsourced customer-service worker for a large delivery-and-retail platform. He can see assigned tickets, template replies, callback records, limited order summaries, and quality review notes, but he cannot access full logistics routes, payment backends, security logs, or management decisions.

## Protagonist Engine

Limited access, careful process reading, ordinary records, timing, and small human misreads.

The protagonist should not win through omniscience or direct authority. He gains leverage by noticing contradictions, asking narrow questions, preserving records, delaying unsafe actions, and making higher-authority people react.

## Main Pressure

The platform treats every abnormal ticket as service error, fraud risk, or audit liability. The protagonist must act on future-dated warnings while leaving a plausible process trail.

## Anomaly Premise

Some customer-service tickets appear with a submission time shortly after the coming midnight. They describe complaints or refund reasons connected to events that have not happened yet.

The anomaly is not a full prophecy system. It gives incomplete service-facing information only.

## What The Story Is Not

- Not a system-panel power fantasy.
- Not a police procedural.
- Not a full cyberpunk corporate war at the opening stage.
- Not an omniscient prediction story.
- Not a workplace process log with no emotional or causal turn.
