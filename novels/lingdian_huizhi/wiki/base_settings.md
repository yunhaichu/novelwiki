## Genre Foundation

Primary genre: modern urban suspense.

Secondary genre if any: light anomaly.

Reader promise: the reader follows a low-authority worker who uses limited process access, timestamps, callback records, and small contradictions to act before visible losses occur.

Setting must support: weak access, process boundaries, audit risk, incomplete warning information, practical pressure, and visible leverage.

Setting must not replace: protagonist choice, reader-entry clarity, scene action, or causal consequence.

## Identity And Authority Rules

- Rule: Chen Lu is an outsourced night-shift customer-service worker, not an internal platform manager.
  Applies to: Chen Lu.
  Writing use: He can handle assigned tickets, see limited order summaries, use reply templates, request callbacks, and leave service notes.
  Limit: He cannot directly change payment status, dispatch logistics, delete records, access security logs, command internal teams, or contact police as a platform authority.

- Rule: Team leads can assign tickets and judge service quality, but they do not control platform-wide data or logistics operations.
  Applies to: customer-service team lead.
  Writing use: Team leads create pressure through quality review, overtime, reassignment, blame, and escalation refusal.
  Limit: They cannot explain anomaly mechanics or resolve major incidents alone.

- Rule: Internal platform departments have higher authority but respond through process, not instant personal trust.
  Applies to: logistics, risk control, payment, security, compliance.
  Writing use: Higher departments can create delay, denial, audit, and suspicion.
  Limit: They should require records, ticket IDs, screenshots, logs, or formal escalation paths before acting.

## Organization And Process Rules

- Rule: A customer-service ticket is valid only through visible support.
  Applies to: all ticket-based actions.
  Required support: ticket ID, submission time, customer account, order number, service category, callback or reply record.
  Writing use: The protagonist's leverage comes from preserving and comparing these supports.
  Limit: A ticket alone does not prove the future or force other departments to act.

- Rule: Escalation requires a plausible service reason.
  Applies to: risk control, logistics, payment, and security escalation.
  Required support: abnormal complaint wording, repeated customer contact, delivery risk, payment dispute, fraud suspicion, or safety concern.
  Writing use: Chen Lu must frame action as service-process concern, not future knowledge.
  Limit: Escalation can be refused, delayed, or turned into audit suspicion.

- Rule: Quality audit punishes abnormal handling unless the worker leaves a defensible process trail.
  Applies to: Chen Lu and night-shift customer-service team.
  Required support: saved notes, call attempt, template reason, supervisor acknowledgment, or visible customer risk.
  Writing use: Every useful action should also create audit exposure.
  Limit: Audit pressure should not become repetitive background; it should force specific choices.

## Resource And Object Rules

- Rule: Assigned tickets are the protagonist's main early resource.
  Applies to: future-dated tickets, normal tickets, reopened tickets.
  Source / holder: platform service system assigns them to Chen Lu's queue.
  Writing use: Tickets provide limited information and process leverage.
  Limit: They contain service-facing fragments, not full event causes.

- Rule: Timestamps and callback records are stronger than the protagonist's explanation.
  Applies to: service records, callback attempts, customer replies, ticket history.
  Source / holder: platform system records visible service actions.
  Writing use: Records allow Chen Lu to prove an inconsistency without revealing the anomaly.
  Limit: Records can also expose that he acted before he should have known.

- Rule: Screenshots and copied notes are risky evidence.
  Applies to: any saved ticket content.
  Source / holder: Chen Lu may preserve limited evidence, but doing so can violate rules.
  Writing use: Evidence can protect against memory or record changes, but increases audit risk.
  Limit: Do not let copied evidence become a clean solution without consequence.

## Space And Location Rules

- Rule: The opening workspace is a night-shift customer-service floor or remote service station with low visibility and high log traceability.
  Applies to: chapter 001 opening.
  Access / route / restriction: Chen Lu can access his workstation and assigned queue only.
  Writing use: Physical isolation supports suspense; system traceability creates pressure.
  Limit: Do not expand into corporate headquarters, police office, or logistics control room during the opening unless the story earns access.

- Rule: Off-platform locations enter the story through tickets, calls, addresses, delivery notes, and later investigation.
  Applies to: customer addresses, delivery points, shops, stations, residential compounds.
  Access / route / restriction: Chen Lu first knows them through service records, not direct observation.
  Writing use: This keeps the opening anchored in limited knowledge.
  Limit: The narration should not confirm off-platform facts as objective truth before a character verifies them.

## Social Life And Language Rules

- Rule: Service dialogue should be practical, defensive, impatient, and process-bound.
  Applies to: Chen Lu, customers, supervisors, internal departments.
  Writing use: Characters should speak through complaint, delay, blame avoidance, request, refusal, or narrow instruction.
  Limit: Avoid rule-manual dialogue and abstract destiny language.

- Rule: Modern workplace pressure should be concrete.
  Applies to: all early chapters.
  Writing use: Use overtime, quality scores, complaint rate, reassignment, frozen escalation, audit note, missed callback, or blame chain.
  Limit: Do not replace workplace pressure with vague terms such as fate, old order, hidden current, or system backside.

- Rule: Chen Lu's immediate personal stake is monthly quality score pressure.
  Applies to: chapter 001 opening.
  Writing use: He already has one unresolved quality warning this month. Another abnormal handling record may cost his night-shift allowance and make rent difficult.
  Limit: This should appear as one practical pressure detail, not as a long backstory.

## Power / Anomaly / Goldfinger Rules

- Mechanism: future-dated service tickets.
  Reader-known rule: some tickets appear with a submission time shortly after the coming midnight and contain complaint information about events that have not happened yet.
  Protagonist-known rule: Chen Lu can see the abnormal ticket after it appears in his assigned queue; he does not know who sends it or why.
  Other-character visible result: other characters may see only odd handling, early concern, unusual notes, or record inconsistencies.
  Source or binding: the anomaly is bound to platform customer-service tickets and Chen Lu's assigned queue at the current stage.
  Current-stage ability: it gives incomplete service-facing warning fragments.
  Current-stage limit: it does not provide full cause, full location certainty, full responsible party, or guaranteed solution.
  Failure mode: wrong interpretation, audit exposure, delayed escalation, customer denial, record change, or action creating a new problem.
  Counter / pressure: platform audit, supervisor suspicion, incomplete records, time pressure, and higher-department refusal.
  Forbidden uses: no full future timeline, no instant identification of culprit, no direct control of events, no public proof of anomaly, no clean police solution at opening stage.
  Disclosure stage: opening shows only one or two concrete abnormal tickets and their immediate pressure.

## Knowledge Boundary Rules

- Reader knows: the ticket timestamp is abnormal when the chapter shows it clearly.
  Protagonist knows: what his queue, ticket content, and service tools show.
  Other characters know: only what Chen Lu tells them or what platform records show.
  Institutions can verify: ticket records, call attempts, order summaries, and escalation notes, not the truth of the future.
  Unknown remains: source of the tickets, selection rule, long-term cost, whether records can change, and whether the warnings can be prevented.

## Forbidden Setting Moves

1. Do not let Chen Lu directly access full logistics, payment, or security backend without a granted path.
2. Do not let the future-dated ticket provide complete event truth.
3. Do not let other characters understand the anomaly without evidence.
4. Do not resolve early incidents through police or management authority without process support.
5. Do not make audit risk disappear after Chen Lu acts.
6. Do not add new anomaly functions, side effects, or costs without an approved wiki update.
7. Do not turn the opening into a platform-process manual.
8. Do not import cyberpunk corporate-war scale before the story earns it.
