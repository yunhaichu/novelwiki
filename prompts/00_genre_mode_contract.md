# Genre Mode Contract Prompt

Use this prompt after project scope / type promise and before major conflict engine, protagonist growth track, and chapter design.

The purpose is to prevent generic governance prompts from dragging every story into the same organization-process-conflict shape.

Each genre has its own reader promise, pressure carrier, growth asset, scene rhythm, and forbidden overuse. The general workflow must be filtered through the current genre mode.

## Core Principle

```text
Universal plot rules must be applied through genre mode.
```

A workplace story may use process, responsibility, approval, KPI, and evidence.

A traditional xianxia story may use sect rules and resources, but should not turn into office workflow. It should prioritize cultivation, dao heart, aptitude, technique access, spiritual resource, master-disciple fate, public standing, trial, combat, and dangerous opportunity.

A romance story should not be converted into organization leverage.

A travel adventure should not be converted into institutional procedure.

## Required Inputs

Read:

- user premise;
- type promise / genre declaration;
- base settings;
- selected reference settings;
- current novel style file if present;
- user constraints.

## Traditional Xianxia Guidance

For traditional xianxia, prefer:

- visible cultivation pressure;
- sect rank and public standing;
- technique access;
- spiritual resource scarcity;
- master / disciple / lineage possibility;
- trial, assessment, mission, contest, injury, forbidden ground, spirit beast, medicine, tool, or dao-heart choice;
- small usable gains such as a technique clue, a minor spiritual object, a senior's attention, a bodily cultivation change, a combat lesson, a debt, a place access, or a public status shift.

Use organization pressure, but do not let it dominate as bureaucracy.

Avoid:

- turning sect life into office approval workflow;
- overusing records, signatures, documents, forms, logs, or procedural liability;
- making every chapter about resource allocation disputes;
- beginning with too many realm tables;
- making humiliation loops without cultivation gain, status change, information, or concrete leverage;
- giving rare treasures without price, danger, debt, or pursuit.

## Checks

Mark `REVISE` if:

- the contract gives generic plot rules without genre filtering;
- the protagonist growth assets do not match the genre;
- the opening chapter pressure carrier is off-genre;
- the story mode drifts toward office / governance / investigation when the genre promise is not that;
- organization pressure overwhelms the genre's primary appeal;
- the terminology budget is too high for opening reader entry;
- the contract does not list genre-specific reality checks.
