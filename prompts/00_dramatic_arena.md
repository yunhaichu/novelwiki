# Dramatic Arena Prompt

Use this prompt after `prompts/00_major_conflict_engine.md`.

The dramatic arena translates macro conflict into a concrete operating system of organizations, locations, resources, records, rules, and characters.

Do not write chapter plot yet.
Do not add many factions just for richness.
Do not make every faction equally important.

## Core Principle

A living story world is not made by more setting details. It is made by active forces that want, fear, hide, misjudge, and react.

Use this structure:

```text
2-3 major forces
+ 3-5 organization-level forces
+ key character actors
```

Major forces provide direction.
Organizations provide friction.
Characters provide choices.

## Frequency Rule

Do not assume every level appears equally in each chapter.

```text
Character emergence: frequent, every chapter.
Organization emergence: occasional, visible at pressure points.
Major-force emergence: rare, mostly as gravity, orders, prices, records, rumors, troop movement, law, or delayed consequences.
```

## Required Inputs

Read:

- major conflict engine;
- base settings;
- type contract / genre promise;
- any approved protagonist premise;
- reference settings if needed.

## Emergence Rule

Each chapter should introduce one pressure into the arena and simulate:

1. which organization must react;
2. which character sees only part of the truth;
3. which character makes a locally rational but globally dangerous choice;
4. what new leverage the protagonist gains;
5. who must respond next.

## Checks

Mark `REVISE` if:

- there are too many forces for the first volume;
- organizations overlap in function;
- a major force directly solves too many local problems;
- characters are only spokespeople for organizations;
- no organization controls concrete resources, records, access, money, violence, legitimacy, or information;
- no character has a private desire that can conflict with affiliation logic;
- the protagonist has no plausible path to affect the arena.
