# Dramatic Arena Prompt

Use this prompt after `prompts/00_major_conflict_engine.md`.

The dramatic arena translates macro conflict into a concrete operating system of organizations, locations, resources, rules, agents, public legitimacy, hidden interests, and characters.

Do not write chapter plot yet.
Do not add many factions just for richness.
Do not make every faction equally important.

## Core Principle

A living story world is not made by more setting details. It is made by active forces that want, fear, hide, misjudge, package their interests, and react through concrete agents.

Use this structure:

```text
2-3 major forces
+ 3-5 organization-level forces
+ key character actors
```

Major forces provide direction.
Organizations provide friction and packaging.
Characters provide choices.

Organizations should not be written as cold abstract interest machines. Each organization needs a public legitimacy mask, real interests, operating boundaries, action ladder, embodied agents, and responsibility-cutting methods.

## Frequency Rule

Do not assume every level appears equally in each chapter.

```text
Character emergence: frequent, every chapter.
Organization emergence: occasional, visible at pressure points.
Major-force emergence: rare, mostly as gravity, orders, prices, records, rumors, troop movement, law, or delayed consequences.
```

## Required Inputs

Read:

- major conflict engine;
- base settings;
- type contract / genre promise;
- any approved protagonist premise;
- `prompts/00_organization_behavior_model.md` when designing organizations;
- reference settings if needed.

## Emergence Rule

Each chapter should introduce one pressure into the arena and simulate:

1. which organization must react;
2. what public packaging that organization uses;
3. which embodied agent appears or acts;

## Checks

Mark `REVISE` if:

- there are too many forces for the first volume;
- organizations overlap in function;
- a major force directly solves too many local problems;
- organizations have naked interests but no public legitimacy mask;
- organizations have no action ladder;
- organizations can jump directly to illegal action without trigger, agent, or responsibility-cutting method;
- organizations act without embodied agents;
- organizations speak in abstract villain language rather than institutional packaging;
- characters are only spokespeople for organizations;
- no organization controls concrete resources, people, objects, access, money, violence, legitimacy, or information;
- no character has a private desire that can conflict with affiliation logic;
- the protagonist has no plausible path to affect the arena.
