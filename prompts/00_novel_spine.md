# Novel Spine Prompt

Use this prompt before writing a new novel or rebuilding a stalled novel.

The purpose is to define the upper-level direction of the whole book without deciding specific chapter events.

This is a strong boundary, not a plot outline.

## Core Principle

The novel spine should answer what the book is fundamentally about.

It should not decide how every chapter happens.

A good spine gives the story direction while still allowing scenes to emerge through character interaction.

## Inputs

Read or ask for:

- initial premise
- intended genre or reader expectation
- protagonist seed
- world pressure
- forbidden directions
- tone preference
- comparable works if useful

## Output Object

Produce:

```text
Novel title:
One-sentence core:
Primary story promise:
Protagonist starting state:
Protagonist long-term change:
Main world pressure:
Central contradiction:
What the story is not:
Final payoff direction:
Non-negotiable boundaries:
Open creative space:
```

## Field Guidance

### One-sentence core

State the book's central movement in one sentence.

Do not include chapter events.

### Primary story promise

What does the reader follow the book for?

Examples:

- survival from below
- relationship repair
- status reversal
- truth discovery
- power growth
- institutional struggle
- family restoration

### Protagonist long-term change

Describe a state change, not a list of achievements.

Example:

```text
random expendable worker -> person with a stable position and leverage
```

### Main world pressure

Name the large force pressing on the protagonist.

It can be a system, route, law, family burden, institution, social order, enemy, or environment.

### Central contradiction

The contradiction should keep producing scenes.

Example:

```text
The protagonist needs to hide their knowledge, but survival requires acting on that knowledge.
```

### What the story is not

Use this to prevent drift.

Do not overfill it.

### Open creative space

List what should remain free for later emergence.

## Rules

- Do not write chapter summaries.
- Do not write beat tables.
- Do not decide scene solutions.
- Do not create a rigid plot cage.
- Keep the spine short enough to remember.
- Preserve room for character-agent interaction.

## Output

Output only the novel spine.
