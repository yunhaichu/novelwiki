# Novel Spine Prompt

Use this prompt before writing a new novel or rebuilding a stalled novel.

The purpose is to define the upper-level direction of the whole book without deciding specific chapter events.

This is a strong boundary, not a plot outline.

## Core Principle

The novel spine should answer what the book is fundamentally about.

It should not decide how every chapter happens.

A good spine gives the story direction while still allowing scenes to emerge through character interaction.

## Control-Based Protagonist Principle

For long-form web fiction, avoid making the protagonist's main appeal rely only on:

- endless power escalation;
- repeated passive suffering;
- constant humiliation repayment;
- larger and larger enemies without a new kind of satisfaction.

Prefer a control-based protagonist engine:

```text
The protagonist becomes more able to understand the situation, influence others, use rules, borrow force, mislead opponents, set traps, withdraw at the right time, and act decisively when action is necessary.
```

Power may grow, but power should not be the only progress.

Pressure and setbacks may happen, but the protagonist should not remain a passive abuse target.

The ideal satisfaction is:

```text
The protagonist may not be the strongest person in the room, but they increasingly understand the room, the rules, the fears, the incentives, and the moment when force is the correct answer.
```

## Inputs

Read or ask for:

- initial premise
- intended genre or reader expectation
- protagonist seed
- world pressure
- forbidden directions
- tone preference
- comparable works if useful

## Output Object

Produce:

```text
Novel title:
One-sentence core:
Primary story promise:
Protagonist starting state:
Protagonist long-term change:
Protagonist engine:
Primary satisfaction:
Control growth ladder:
Force rule:
Suffering rule:
Power growth rule:
Main world pressure:
Central contradiction:
What the story is not:
Final payoff direction:
Non-negotiable boundaries:
Open creative space:
```

## Field Guidance

### One-sentence core

State the book's central movement in one sentence.

Do not include chapter events.

### Primary story promise

What does the reader follow the book for?

Examples:

- survival from below
- relationship repair
- status reversal
- truth discovery
- power growth
- institutional struggle
- family restoration
- control from below
- strategic counterplay

### Protagonist long-term change

Describe a state change, not a list of achievements.

Example:

```text
random expendable worker -> person with a stable position and leverage
```

### Protagonist engine

Describe how the protagonist creates satisfaction.

Prefer precise engines such as:

```text
control / strategy / leverage / rule use / relationship influence / survival judgment / selective force
```

Avoid vague labels such as:

```text
smart protagonist
strong protagonist
cool protagonist
```

### Primary satisfaction

Name what the reader repeatedly gets.

Examples:

```text
The protagonist reads a situation before others do.
The protagonist makes an opponent's move help them.
The protagonist gives ground on the surface while preserving the key condition.
The protagonist acts directly when restraint would become weakness.
```

### Control growth ladder

Define 4 to 6 stages of growing control.

Example:

```text
1. can protect self briefly
2. can influence one person
3. can use one local rule
4. can make a small group move in a useful direction
5. can make an opponent misread the situation
6. can decide when to use force and when to withhold it
```

### Force rule

State when the protagonist should act directly.

The protagonist should not solve every problem with force, but when force is the correct answer, action should be decisive.

### Suffering rule

Pressure is allowed. Repeated passive abuse is not.

If the protagonist suffers, it should create information, leverage, relationship movement, changed position, or a future counterplay.

### Power growth rule

Power may grow, but power growth should support the protagonist engine rather than replace it.

### Main world pressure

Name the large force pressing on the protagonist.

It can be a system, route, law, family burden, institution, social order, enemy, or environment.

### Central contradiction

The contradiction should keep producing scenes.

Example:

```text
The protagonist needs to hide their knowledge, but survival requires acting on that knowledge.
```

### What the story is not

Use this to prevent drift.

Do not overfill it.

### Open creative space

List what should remain free for later emergence.

## Rules

- Do not write chapter summaries.
- Do not write beat tables.
- Do not decide scene solutions.
- Do not create a rigid plot cage.
- Keep the spine short enough to remember.
- Preserve room for character-agent interaction.
- Define the protagonist's satisfaction engine before defining long-term events.
- Avoid both pure power inflation and long passive suffering as the main engine.

## Output

Output only the novel spine.
