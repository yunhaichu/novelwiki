# Reality-Causal Preflight Prompt

Use this prompt after `prompts/00_genre_mode_contract.md` and before major conflict engine, dramatic arena, protagonist growth track, and chapter design.

The purpose is to prevent scenes that feel dramatic but fail the internal logic of the genre world, industry, craft, institution, body, economy, or social setting.

This is a pre-writing step. Do not wait until draft review to discover that a doctor would not appear, a technician would not repair that part, a sect would not run that process, or a protagonist could not physically survive the proposed training.

## Core Principle

```text
A plot event must be possible before it can be dramatic.
```

Every genre has a reality layer. It may be real-world business logic, cultivation ecology, sect discipline, court bureaucracy, military logistics, school rules, criminal procedure, village social order, or magical law.

The story may be fictional, but its local causality must hold.

## Required Inputs

Read:

- genre mode contract;
- base settings;
- selected reference settings;
- major conflict engine if already drafted;
- organization behavior model if already drafted;
- character behavior model if already drafted;
- current protagonist growth track if already drafted;
- user constraints.

## Output Format

```text
# Reality-Causal Preflight

Novel ID:
Chapter / arc target:
Genre mode:
Source files read:

## 1. Genre Reality

What is the underlying reality of this genre?
-

What resources matter?
-

How are resources produced, distributed, consumed, or lost?
-

How do low-level characters survive?
-

What does growth require physically, socially, economically, spiritually, or institutionally?
-

What constraints cannot be handwaved?
-

## 2. Normal Process

For the proposed event type, how would it normally happen in this world?
-

Who appears first?
-

Who has authority?
-

Who knows what?
-

Who does not know what?
-

What actions would be natural?
-

What actions would be unnatural unless justified?
-

## 3. Large Trend Transmission

What larger trend is pressing down?
-

How does the trend reach mid-level organizations or groups?
-

What would these organizations naturally do first?
-

What is the lowest-cost response for each organization?
-

What would be too extreme too early?
-

## 4. Chapter Event Feasibility

Proposed chapter event:
-

Would this event still happen if the protagonist were absent?
-

Why now?
-

Who has motive?
-

Who has authority?
-

Who has information?
-

Who can execute it?
-

Who would resist?
-

What must be changed to make this event natural?
-

## 5. Character Position Feasibility

For each key actor:

### Actor
Name / role:
Why are they present?
What do they realistically know?
What do they realistically want now?
What action is natural for them?
What action would be forced or author-convenient?
What environmental or social constraint affects them?

## 6. Object Function Test

Key object / trace / document / place:
-

Why does it matter in this genre reality?
-

Who would care about it?
-

Who would move, protect, take, hide, inspect, or destroy it?
-

Can it change action, access, status, resource, relationship, or danger?
-

Is it naturally important, or is the author forcing importance onto it?
-

If removed, does the chapter still work?
-

## 7. Cost / Survival Test

What does the protagonist pay?
-

Can the protagonist physically / socially / economically / spiritually survive this cost?
-

What makes the growth possible despite the cost?
-

What prevents easy success?
-

Does the cost fit the protagonist's current stage?
-

## 8. Result Attractor

What state should the chapter end in?

Do not specify exact dialogue. Specify situation state.

- protagonist state:
- object / resource state:
- relationship state:
- organization state:
- danger state:
- unresolved pressure:

## 9. Preflight Decision

ALLOW / REVISE

Required changes before drafting:
1.
2.
3.
```

## Traditional Xianxia Preflight Examples

For traditional xianxia, check:

- how spirit roots are tested and categorized;
- why a weak or strange result would still be kept, rejected, or placed into trial service;
- why the sect benefits from keeping low-quality candidates;
- what low-level cultivators eat, do, and survive on;
- how basic cultivation differs from village or rogue cultivator methods;
- whether time limits are physically and spiritually plausible;
- whether labor leaves any time and strength for practice;
- whether a minor gain is cultivation-appropriate rather than bureaucratic;
- whether an object is naturally spiritual or artificially made into a talisman;
- why a senior, peer, or institution notices the protagonist.

## Checks

Mark `REVISE` if:

- the event only exists because the author needs it;
- a person or organization appears without a natural entry point;
- a key object is important only because the plot says so;
- the protagonist survives impossible cost without mechanism;
- the protagonist grows despite lack of time, food, energy, training, resource, or opportunity;
- the chapter's result attractor does not follow from the normal process;
- an institution acts against its own cost logic;
- a high-level actor acts too early;
- the proposed scene violates the genre mode's own reality.
