# Major Conflict Engine Prompt

Use this prompt after base settings and before dramatic arena design.

The goal is to define the large structural conflict that will keep the story moving. Do not write a detailed chapter outline. Do not pre-decide specific events beyond the initial trigger or first pressure source.

## Core Principle

A major conflict engine defines why the world cannot remain still.

It should describe an unstable structure, not a sequence of plot events.

Good major conflict:

```text
Two or more large forces need each other, fear each other, and cannot let each other win.
```

Bad major conflict:

```text
A villain wants power.
A protagonist wants justice.
They fight.
```

## Required Inputs

Read:

- user premise;
- type contract if present;
- base settings;
- genre promise;
- any explicit constraints about protagonist, world, power system, time period, or tone.

## Checks

Mark `REVISE` if:

- the conflict can be solved by one honest conversation;
- one side is purely evil or purely irrational;
- the two sides do not depend on each other;
- no recurring conflict fronts exist;
- the trigger is too abstract for a first chapter;
- the macro conflict requires constant exposition to be understood;
- the protagonist has no plausible position where macro pressure can reach them.
