# Organization Behavior Model Prompt

Use this prompt when creating or upgrading an organization, faction, company, bureau, sect, clan, gang, school, hospital, guild, state agency, state-level force, or any collective actor.

The purpose is to prevent organizations from becoming cold abstract machines that act only for plot convenience.

Organizations have interests, but they usually do not state those interests nakedly. They preserve legitimacy by packaging control, extraction, coercion, research, punishment, and secrecy as public goods.

## Hard Source Rule

All organization behavior must come from approved sources:

- user initial premise / initial settings;
- current novel wiki;
- base settings;
- major conflict engine;
- dramatic arena;
- approved organization files;
- approved chapter states.

If a motivation, action ladder, internal faction, illegal method, cover language, or cutout relationship is not supported, mark an `organization_gap` instead of inventing it silently.

## Core Principle

```text
People have personality masks.
Organizations have legitimacy masks.
```

An organization has:

```text
public legitimacy
+ real interests
+ operating boundaries
+ packaging language
+ action ladder
+ internal factions
+ embodied agents
+ environmental modulation
+ responsibility-cutting methods
```

## Checks

Mark `REVISE` if:

- the organization only has a naked interest and no public legitimacy mask;
- the organization can do anything without boundary;
- the organization jumps directly to illegal action without action ladder or trigger;
- the organization acts without embodied agents;
- the organization speaks in abstract villain language rather than institutional packaging;
- every member of the organization acts as one mind;
- internal factions or frontline executor constraints are ignored when relevant;
- the organization has no way to cut responsibility;
- the organization cannot misjudge anything;
- the organization does not change behavior between public, private, strong target, weak target, media presence, superior presence, or evidence exposure.
