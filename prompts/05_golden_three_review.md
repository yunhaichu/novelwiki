# Golden Three Review Prompt

Use this prompt for a new story opening or a new major arc opening.

This is a special retention review. It does not replace the normal chapter review.

## When To Use

Use before drafting chapters 1-3:

- to review the golden-three design
- to check whether the opening has enough reader pull

Use again after chapters 1-3 are drafted:

- to review the three chapters as one opening unit
- to decide whether the opening can proceed or needs structural revision

## Inputs

Read:

- current novel project file
- current novel style file
- current arc outline or opening outline
- chapter 1-3 drafts if available
- relevant character files
- relevant world files
- `governance/reader_reward_check.md`
- `governance/emotional_payoff_check.md`
- `governance/anti_ai_taste_check.md`

## Core Question

After three chapters, does the reader clearly know:

- what the protagonist wants
- why the protagonist cannot easily get it
- what makes the protagonist worth following
- what visible gain or change has already happened
- what larger possibility has opened
- why the next chapter is worth reading

If not, mark REVISE.

## Chapter 1 Checks

Chapter 1 should establish:

- protagonist desire
- immediate obstacle
- core pressure or deadline
- why the protagonist is disadvantaged
- one small usable trait, habit, skill, or perspective
- a visible sign that the protagonist is not completely invisible
- a reason to read chapter 2

Mark REVISE if chapter 1 spends too long on setup before the core pressure appears.

## Chapter 2 Checks

Chapter 2 should establish:

- the pressure from chapter 1 is real
- the protagonist makes an active choice
- the choice has a visible cost
- the choice creates a visible gain
- someone notices the gain
- the chapter does not read like a daily routine or process log

Mark REVISE if chapter 2 only adds hardship without a visible reader reward.

## Chapter 3 Checks

Chapter 3 should establish:

- the gain from chapter 2 expands or changes the situation
- the gain creates a new complication
- a relationship shifts or gains tension
- the protagonist is still limited, but the path forward is clearer
- the long-term arc has opened
- chapter 4 has a concrete hook

Mark REVISE if chapter 3 resets into another local task without widening the story.

## Opening Unit Checks

The three chapters together should contain:

- clear reader motivation
- visible reader reward
- rising stakes
- at least one relationship shift
- one concrete long-term hook
- no major genre drift
- no repeated task-of-the-day structure
- no protagonist solving everything cleanly
- no excessive hidden payoff
- no dialogue that feels like characters are only asking reader questions

## Reader Reward Ledger

For the opening as a whole, fill in:

```text
Reader expectation:
Main obstacle:
Protagonist's active pattern:
Visible gain by chapter 3:
Who notices:
Larger possibility opened:
Reason to continue:
```

## Decision

Return one of:

- PASS
- REVISE

## Output Format

```text
Decision: PASS / REVISE

Opening diagnosis:

Strongest hook:

Weakest retention risk:

Required structural fixes:
1.
2.
3.
```

Keep fixes structural. Do not list minor line edits unless they affect retention.
