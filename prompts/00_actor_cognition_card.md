# Actor Cognition Card Prompt

Use this prompt after dramatic arena design and before chapter emergence design.

The goal is to prevent all characters, organizations, and factions from acting as if they are all-knowing. Plot should emerge from limited viewpoint, partial evidence, self-interest, and reasonable mistakes.

## Core Principle

Characters cannot act from truth.
They can only act from what they can see, believe, afford, fear, and misunderstand.

Good plot often comes from choices that are locally rational but globally dangerous.

## Required Inputs

Read:

- major conflict engine;
- dramatic arena;
- relevant character file or planned character role;
- relevant organization file;
- current volume state if present.

## Output Format

```text
# Actor Cognition Card

Actor name:
Actor type: character / organization / major force
Affiliation:
Narrative level: major-force / organization / character

## Position

What position does this actor occupy in the world?
-

What can this actor realistically affect?
-

What is outside this actor's reach?
-

## Direct Knowledge

What can this actor directly see, verify, inspect, hear, command, or touch?
-

What evidence does this actor trust most?
-

What evidence does this actor distrust?
-

## Indirect Knowledge

What does this actor only know through reports, rumor, records, agents, subordinates, superiors, or hearsay?
-

Which channels may distort this knowledge?
-

## Unknowns

What does this actor absolutely not know right now?
-

What does the reader know that this actor does not know?
-

What does this actor think they know but may be wrong about?
-

## Interest And Time Scale

Immediate interest:
-

Long-term interest:
-

Fear:
-

What cost can this actor bear?
-

What cost can this actor not bear?
-

Time scale: immediate survival / daily operation / promotion / institutional survival / dynastic / strategic

## Default Judgment Logic

When under pressure, this actor tends to interpret events as:
-

This actor's first instinct is to:
-

This actor's default mistake is:
-

## Local Rational Choice

Under pressure, what choice would be reasonable from this actor's limited view?
-

Why is it reasonable to them?
-

Why may it be wrong globally?
-

Who is harmed by this choice?
-

What future reaction does this choice trigger?
-

## Anti-Omniscience Rules

This actor must not:
-

This actor should not know:
-

This actor should not correctly infer without evidence:
-
```

## Checks

Mark `REVISE` if:

- the actor knows information outside their position;
- the actor makes the globally optimal choice without sufficient evidence;
- the actor acts only to help or block the protagonist;
- the actor has no private interest beyond their faction's abstract goal;
- the actor's mistake is random rather than rooted in position, fear, evidence, or incentive;
- the actor can bear any cost without changing behavior.

## Use In Chapters

Before writing an important scene, identify:

```text
What each actor knows.
What each actor wants.
What each actor fears.
What each actor misreads.
What each actor cannot say openly.
```

Dialogue and action should come from these limits.
