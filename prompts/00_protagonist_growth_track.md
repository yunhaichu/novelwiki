# Protagonist Growth Track Prompt

Use this prompt after project scope, type promise, base settings, major conflict engine, dramatic arena, character behavior model, and organization behavior model are available.

The purpose is to define the protagonist's controlled growth path.

The world should not revolve around the protagonist. Major forces move according to their own interests. Organizations transfer and package pressure according to their own logic. Other characters act from local viewpoints.

The author's invisible hand should not solve problems for the protagonist. It should only place the protagonist at growth-relevant pressure points.

## Core Principle

```text
From large to small: the larger trend is hard to resist.
From small to large: characters and scenes emerge naturally.
Control line: the protagonist grows through pressure, cost, usable leverage, and repeated application.
```

The protagonist should not develop randomly.

The protagonist should not jump levels.

Each stage should expand what the protagonist can perceive, survive, access, influence, or change.

## Invisible Hand Rule

The author's invisible hand may:

- place the protagonist where a growth-relevant pressure is about to land;
- let the protagonist encounter problems that expose their current weakness;
- ensure costs produce at least one usable lever;
- make repeated pressure test whether a previous lesson was learned;
- let other actors notice the protagonist only after scene action justifies it.

The author's invisible hand must not:

- make the protagonist win without cost;
- give the protagonist information they did not earn;
- make stronger organizations act stupidly for the protagonist;
- let the protagonist skip a growth stage;
- let a single insight solve a structural problem;
- turn support characters into tools that exist only to hand the protagonist leverage.

## Chapter Growth Contract

Each chapter involving the protagonist should answer:

1. Which growth stage is active?
2. What pressure tests the protagonist's current weakness?
3. What choice does the protagonist make?
4. What cost is paid?
5. What usable leverage is gained?
6. What remains unsolved?
7. What future stage does this prepare?

## Checks

Mark `REVISE` if:

- the protagonist has no staged growth path;
- the protagonist gains power without cost;
- the protagonist learns a lesson but cannot use it later;
- the protagonist receives leverage from coincidence rather than scene action;
- the protagonist skips from survival to control too early;
- the protagonist solves the large trend instead of surviving its pressure;
- each chapter pushes plot but not protagonist capability, access, relationship, judgment, or leverage;
- support characters exist only to upgrade the protagonist;
- the protagonist's growth contradicts the character behavior model without threshold or aftermath support.
