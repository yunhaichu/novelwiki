# Power / Anomaly / Goldfinger Base Laws

Use this reference only when the project explicitly includes a power system, goldfinger, anomaly, supernatural force, system panel, rebirth memory, prophecy, rule-based ability, or technology ability.

This file is a reference setting base. It is not canon for any novel.

## Selection Rule

Do not apply this file to a purely realistic project unless the user or approved project spine explicitly includes a special mechanism.

If the project says there is no goldfinger, no supernatural element, no special ability, and no anomaly, do not import these rules as hidden mechanics.

## Base Laws

### Reader Understandability Law

Any ability or anomaly used to solve a key conflict must have its basic rule visible to the reader before or during its use.

Use:

- prevent sudden cheats
- make payoff legible
- let readers anticipate future use

### Stable Boundary Law

At the same story stage, what the mechanism can and cannot do should remain stable.

Use:

- prevent contradiction
- let failure feel fair
- allow opponents or systems to adapt

### Limit-First Law

The limit, condition, cost, exposure risk, countermeasure, or narrowness of the mechanism is more important than a long list of functions.

Use:

- preserve pressure
- prevent one ability from deleting all conflict

### Visible Result Law

A mechanism should create an observable result: changed object, record, injury, missing memory, altered route, public reaction, resource movement, blocked access, trace, or new risk.

Use:

- avoid pure internal explanation
- create scene-level payoff

### Old Problem / New Problem Law

A mechanism may solve the current problem, but it should naturally expose a higher-level pressure, new cost, stronger opponent, narrower path, or deeper question.

Use:

- support long-form progression

### Protagonist Agency Law

The mechanism may provide advantage, but the key choice, timing, risk, interpretation, and action should remain with the protagonist.

Use:

- prevent the protagonist from becoming a tool of the system or prophecy

### Genre Promise Law

The same mechanism must express itself through the primary genre promise.

Examples:

- mystery: clue, contradiction, proof chain
- business: information advantage, timing, resource, reputation
- cultivation: resource, realm, rule, survival
- science fiction: technical constraint, evidence, system limit, human cost
- romance: emotional risk, choice, trust, commitment

Use:

- prevent genre drift

### Secret Boundary Law

If the mechanism is secret, define what the reader knows, what the protagonist knows, what other characters observe, and what institutions can verify.

Use:

- prevent knowledge leak
- protect suspense
- avoid other characters magically understanding the mechanism

### Source Binding Law

The mechanism should have at least one binding source: protagonist identity, experience, object, decision, world rule, institution, technology, inheritance, accident, or relationship.

Use:

- reduce arbitrary feeling

### Stage Disclosure Law

Early story should show only functions needed by current pressure. Later chapters can expand the mechanism after cost, evidence, or failure.

Use:

- avoid setting overload
- preserve discovery

## Review Triggers

Mark a plan or draft for revision if:

- a mechanism solves a major conflict before readers understand the basic rule.
- a mechanism gains a new function only because the plot needs it.
- the protagonist no longer makes meaningful choices.
- other characters understand the hidden mechanism without evidence.
- the mechanism has no visible result.
- the mechanism creates no new pressure after use.
- the mechanism imported from one genre overrides the declared primary genre promise.
