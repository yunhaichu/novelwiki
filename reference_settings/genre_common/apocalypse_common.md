# Apocalypse Common Reference

Use this reference for apocalypse, zombie, disaster, wasteland, infection, collapse, evacuation, camp, and survival-order projects.

This file is a reference setting base. It is not canon for any novel.

## Identity And Authority

Common roles:

- ordinary survivor: limited force and limited resource access.
- scavenger: obtains supplies through route risk and information.
- medic: controls treatment priority, medicine knowledge, and triage pressure.
- technician / mechanic: restores equipment, power, vehicles, water, communication, or locks.
- patrol member: holds local security authority but depends on camp rules and trust.
- cleanup team member: faces high-risk outside threats and gains contribution visibility.
- camp registrar / quartermaster: controls records, ration, storage, and allocation.
- convoy leader / temporary commander: controls route, order, evacuation, and tradeoffs.
- black-market broker: exchanges resource, information, weapon, medicine, or passage.

Authority rule:

In collapse settings, authority is usually local, fragile, and resource-backed. A role can command only if others believe it can protect, distribute, punish, record, or provide a path to survival.

## Organization And Process

Common organizations:

- temporary command center: evacuation, emergency orders, shelter control.
- survivor camp: food, shelter, conflict, identity, and internal rules.
- supply registration desk: inventory, ration, contribution, dispute.
- quarantine station: infection fear, exclusion, concealed injury, medical ethics.
- medical point: scarce drugs, triage, dependency.
- patrol team: security, abuse risk, protection.
- cleanup team: danger, contribution, status movement.
- broadcast station: information, rumor, rescue signal, panic.
- black market: gray exchange and betrayal.
- convoy: migration, vehicle, fuel, route, leadership conflict.
- water point / power room / repair group: survival infrastructure.

Common processes:

- supply registration: needs item, amount, record, and witness.
- ration distribution: needs identity, contribution, inventory, and rule.
- infection screening: needs symptom, wound, contact history, test, or authority.
- scavenging mission: needs route, members, goal, and return condition.
- camp entry: needs identity, risk assessment, sponsor, contribution, or quarantine.
- weapon allocation: needs role, trust, inventory, and risk.
- route scouting: needs map, observation, trace, and report.
- emergency evacuation: needs alarm, vehicles, priority, and visible order.
- horde / disaster warning: needs lookout, sound, trace, sensor, broadcast, or witness.
- black-market exchange: needs bargaining chip, trust, location, and risk.
- contribution scoring: needs task result, witness, and record.

Process rule:

A survival process should change access to food, safety, shelter, movement, medicine, weapon, information, or trust.

## Resource And Object

Useful survival resources:

- clean water / food / medicine / fuel / battery
- vehicle / map / radio / generator / solar charger
- weapon / ammunition / protective gear / tool kit
- entry pass / ration card / contribution record
- wound record / quarantine tag / route note / supply list
- flashlight / rope / lockpick / disinfectant / mask

Resource rule:

Scarce resources should have source, amount, holder, distribution logic, and conflict risk. Do not let supplies appear at scale without storage, route, scavenging, trade, production, or seizure.

## Space And Location

Common spaces:

- shelter, camp gate, quarantine zone, storage room, clinic, water point, generator room, vehicle depot, abandoned supermarket, hospital, school, underground parking, highway checkpoint, bridge, rooftop, broadcast room.

Space rule:

Location should define risk: noise, smell, route visibility, choke point, infection exposure, line of sight, lock condition, fuel cost, or retreat path.

## Social Life

Apocalypse pressure often comes from:

- trust scarcity
- resource rationing
- hidden injury or infection suspicion
- contribution ranking
- weak people becoming bargaining chips
- family and group responsibility
- order vs survival conflict
- rumor and panic

Dialogue should often be short, practical, suspicious, bargaining, exhausted, or command-like. Do not make every character explain morality directly.

## Genre Guard

Avoid:

- declaring all order gone everywhere unless the project explicitly requires it.
- making supplies unlimited after one discovery.
- using repeated suffering without information, leverage, relationship movement, or changed route.
- letting camp rules appear only to punish the protagonist.
- treating every survivor as either saint or monster.
- using infection, horde, or disaster only as background mood with no process consequence.
- making the protagonist solve logistics without time, weight, route, storage, or social cost.
