# Failure Case: Polished Role-Reversal Ending

## Symptom

A chapter ends with a clean contrast sentence that explains the protagonist's thematic role instead of landing on a concrete scene consequence.

## Example Pattern

```text
He was not here to be the protagonist.
He was here to stand in the protagonist's way.
```

## Reject Rule

If the ending explains role meaning instead of landing on action, object, danger, cost, or changed relationship, mark:

```text
Prose decision: NEEDS LINE REVISION
```

Do not mark structural failure if the chapter's reader entry, consequence chain, and escalation pass.

## Repair Strategy

End on one of:

- a concrete object pressing into the body;
- the character being ordered forward;
- a visible route, door, token, or weapon;
- a physical inconvenience;
- a changed relationship or command;
- a cost that must be paid in the next scene.

## Expected Decision

```text
Workflow decision: PASS if structure works
Prose decision: NEEDS LINE REVISION
```
